Hello, We hope that you’ve made a good start on your coursework. We wanted to bring a small correction to your attention regarding Question 3 (e). The revised question should read as follows: Q3(e) A tautology is a proposition that is always true. Let p; q and r be three propositions. Using the laws of propositions or the truth table, show whether ((p ∨ q) ∧ (r → s)) ⟺ ((p ∧ r) → s) ∧ ((q ∧ r) → s) is a tautology or not. [4 marks] Please use this version when you answer question 3 (e) in the Mid-term Assessment [001] activity. Thank you for taking the time to read this. Best of luck with the coursework!

Summary of set theory Set theory is a fundamental area of mathematics that studies collections of objects, known as sets. It forms the basis for various mathematical disciplines and has applications in computer science, logic and more. Here's a concise summary of the key concepts and laws in set theory. Basic concepts Definition of a set and how sets are represented. This includes the listing method, the set builder notation method and Venn diagram. Member of a set, cardinality of a set and subset of set. Types of sets: empty set, finite and infinite sets. Powerset of a set. Set operations Union, intersection, set different, and symmetric difference of sets. The complement of a set. Laws of set theory Commutative laws. Associative laws. Distributive laws. Identity laws. Complement laws. Absorption laws. Set difference law. De Morgan’s laws. You also need to know how use the laws of set theory or Venn diagrams to prove a relation between two given sets.

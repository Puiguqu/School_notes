Combinatorics is a branch of mathematics focused on counting, arranging and combining elements in sets. It has wide applications in fields such as computer science, probability, statistics and optimisation. Here are the key concepts we covered in this topic: Basic counting principles Addition principle: If there are a ways to do something and b ways to do another thing, and the two cannot happen simultaneously, there are a+b ways to do either. Multiplication principle: If there are a ways to do something and b ways to do another thing after that, there are a×b ways to do both. Permutations and combinations Permutations: The arrangement of n elements in a specific order. Formula for permutations of n elements: P(n)=n! Formula for permutations of n elements taken r at a time: P(n,r)= (n−r)! n! Combinations:The selection of n elements without regard to order. Formula for combinations of n elements taken r at a time: ( ( r n )= r!(n−r)! n! Binomial theorem Expansion of (x+y) n : (x+y) n =∑ k=0 n ( k n )x n−k y k The coefficients ( ( k n ) are binomial coefficients, representing the number of ways to choose k elements from n elements. Pigeonhole principle Statement: If n items are put into m containers and n>m, then at least one container must contain more than one item. Application: Proving the existence of certain properties within a set. Combinatorics provides the mathematical foundation for counting, arranging and analysing sets and sequences. Combinatorics is an essential tool in many areas of mathematics and applied sciences.

As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Topic 9. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge. 1. Understanding Heap Properties I can explain the *heap property* and how it ensures that each parent node satisfies the required relationship with its children. I can describe the *shape property* and its role in maintaining the structure of a complete binary tree. I can identify whether a given binary tree satisfies both the heap and shape properties. 2. Heap Operations I can describe the insertion operation for a heap and explain how it maintains heap properties. I can outline the deletion process (e.g., removing the root) and demonstrate how the heap is restructured to preserve its properties. I can write pseudocode for common heap operations, including insertion and deletion. 3. Building a Heap I can build a heap in place from an unsorted array. I can explain the process of heapifying a subtree and how it contributes to constructing a valid heap. I can demonstrate the step-by-step process of transforming an array into a heap. 4. Heapsort I understand the heapsort algorithm and how it utilizes the heap structure for sorting. I can implement heapsort in code and explain its logic. I can analyse the time complexity of heapsort and compare it with other sorting algorithms. 5. Hands-On Practice I have successfully implemented a heap and its operations in a programming language. I have tested my implementation to confirm its correctness for various scenarios. I feel confident in applying heap concepts to solve real-world problems or coding challenges. 6. Reflection I can articulate the practical applications of heaps and their operations. I feel prepared to tackle advanced problems involving heaps or heap-based algorithms.

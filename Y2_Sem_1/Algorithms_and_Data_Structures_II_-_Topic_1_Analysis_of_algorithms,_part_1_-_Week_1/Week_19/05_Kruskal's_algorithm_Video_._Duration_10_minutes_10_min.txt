**Kruskal's Algorithm**

## Step 1: Introduction to Kruskal's Algorithm
Kruskal's algorithm is a greedy algorithm used to find the minimum spanning tree (MST) of a connected, undirected, and weighted graph. It was developed by Jaroslav Kruskal in 1956.

## Step 2: How Kruskal's Algorithm Works
The algorithm works by sorting all the edges of the graph in non-decreasing order of their weight. Then, it iterates over each edge in the sorted list and adds an edge to the MST if the edge does not form a cycle with the edges already included in the MST.

## Step 3: Example of Kruskal's Algorithm
Consider a graph with five vertices (A, B, C, D, E) and six edges with weights 1, 2, 2, 4, 5, and 6. The sorted list of edges is [2, 2, 4, 5, 6, 1]. The MST will include the edges with weights 2, 2, and 1.

## Step 4: Time Complexity
The time complexity of Kruskal's algorithm is O(E log E), where E is the number of edges in the graph.

## Step 5: Space Complexity
The space complexity of Kruskal's algorithm is O(E), where E is the number of edges in the graph.

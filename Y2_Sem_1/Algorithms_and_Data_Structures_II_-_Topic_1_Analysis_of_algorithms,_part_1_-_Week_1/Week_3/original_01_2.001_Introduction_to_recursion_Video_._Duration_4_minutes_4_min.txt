Let's begin with the first part, understanding recursion. The simplest definition of a recursive algorithm is an algorithm that calls itself. According to this definition, this algorithm is a recursive algorithm. You can see the algorithm is called, "hello" and it's made up of two instructions. The first instruction is an instruction that prints the word hello on the screen. The second instruction is the very same algorithm hello being called. That is the algorithm hello is called inside the algorithm hello. This is what is known as a recursive call, a call of a function inside the same function. Now, let's trace this simple recursive algorithm to find out what task is performing. I will simulate the execution of the algorithm instruction by instruction, and on the right, I will show you the information that is eventually printed on the screen. The first instruction simply prints the word hello on a screen. The second instruction is a recursive call. In terms of tracing, we can't think of a recursive call us another copy of the same algorithm being activated. While this copy is being executed, the regional algorithm is in a standby waiting for this copy to finish its execution. We then start the execution of the recursive call. As before, the first instruction prints the word hello on a screen. We proceed to the next instruction, which is in turn another recursive call to the same algorithm. Thus we open another copy of the same function, and the execution of the first recursive call is paused, waiting for the second recursive call to finish its execution. As with the previous recursive call, the second recursive call starts by printing the word hello on the screen, then, it proceeds to execute another recursive call. By now, you might have realized that this recursive algorithm is never going to finish its execution. This situation is known as infinite recursion, and it is the result of a badly constructed recursive algorithm. You can try to execute this recursive algorithm on your computer and see that it's execution never ends. Your screen will be filled with the word hello.

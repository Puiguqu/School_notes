As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Week 3. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge. 1. Understanding Recursion I can explain the concept of algorithmic recursion. I understand how a recursive function calls itself and eventually terminates with a base case. I can identify problems that are well-suited for recursive solutions. 2. Writing Recursive Algorithms I can trace a recursive algorithm step-by-step to understand its behaviour and output. I can write a recursive algorithm to solve a problem from scratch. I can convert an iterative algorithm into its recursive version using pseudocode. 3. Analysing Recursive Algorithms I can calculate the time complexity of recursive algorithms using methods like the Master Theorem or recurrence relations. I can explain the trade-offs between recursion and iteration, including considerations of time complexity and memory usage. I can identify and avoid common pitfalls in recursive algorithms, such as infinite recursion or inefficient overlapping subproblems. 4. Application and Practice I have practiced tracing and debugging recursive algorithms. I have successfully solved multiple problems using recursion in both pseudocode and actual code. I feel confident applying recursion to new and unfamiliar problems.

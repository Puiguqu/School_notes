As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Week 4. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge. 1. Understanding Recursive Algorithms I can identify and explain the concept of recursive algorithms. I can trace the execution of a given recursive algorithm step by step. I understand the difference between iterative and recursive approaches to solving problems. 2. Writing Recursive Algorithms I can write recursive algorithms from scratch for given problems. I can convert iterative algorithms into recursive algorithms using pseudocode. I can ensure the correctness of a recursive algorithm by analysing base and recursive cases. 3. Master Theorem and Recurrence Relations I can explain what recurrence equations represent and how they describe recursive algorithms. I can apply the Master Theorem to solve recurrence relations. I can determine the time complexity of a recursive algorithm using recurrence relations. 4. Application and Analysis I can evaluate the efficiency of a recursive algorithm by calculating its time complexity. I can compare the efficiency of recursive and iterative solutions for the same problem. I can identify scenarios where recursion is more effective than iteration and vice versa. 5. Reflection I can articulate how the Master Theorem simplifies the process of analysing recursive algorithms. I feel confident in applying the skills learned to new problems involving recursion and complexity analysis.

Quicksort is one of the two recursive comparison sorting algorithms we will study. In every call, Quicksort chooses a number, the pivot. All numbers lower than the pivot are restored to its left and all numbers higher than the pivot are stored to its right. In that way, after every call, the pivot is stored in its correct position. Let's start the execution of Quicksort with this array. So the first thing we are going to do is choosing the pivot. In this implementation, we will choose the number in the highest position as the pivot. In this case, it's number 17.

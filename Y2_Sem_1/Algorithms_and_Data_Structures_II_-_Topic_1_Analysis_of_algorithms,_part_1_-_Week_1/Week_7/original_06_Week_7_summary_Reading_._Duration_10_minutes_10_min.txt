In this week we focused on exploring the fundamental limitations of comparison-based sorting algorithms and introducing non-comparison sorting techniques. You studied three key non-comparison sorting algorithms: counting sort, radix sort, and bucket sort. You identified the unique approaches of each algorithm, implemented them in practice, and analysed their time complexities. The discussions highlighted how non-comparison sorting algorithms could achieve time complexities better than O(nlogn) O, left parenthesis, n, log, n, right parenthesis under certain conditions, contrasting them with the inherent limits of comparison-based sorting methods. Through hands-on exercises and theoretical exploration, you gained a deeper appreciation of the scenarios where non-comparison sorting algorithms are most effective, broadening their problem-solving toolkit for efficient data organisation.

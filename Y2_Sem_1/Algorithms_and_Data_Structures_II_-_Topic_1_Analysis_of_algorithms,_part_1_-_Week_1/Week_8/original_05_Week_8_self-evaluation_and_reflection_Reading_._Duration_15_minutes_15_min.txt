As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Week 8. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge. 1. Understanding Non-Comparison Sorting Algorithms I can explain the concept of non-comparison sorting algorithms and how they differ from comparison-based sorting methods. I can describe the step-by-step approach of radix sort and how it organises data based on positional values. I can explain the bucket sort process and how it uses buckets to group and sort elements. 2. Implementation Skills I have implemented radix sort and tested it on different datasets. I have implemented bucket sort and tested it on datasets with varying characteristics (e.g., uniform distribution, skewed data). I can write code to dynamically adjust the number of buckets in bucket sort based on input size or range. 3. Time Complexity Analysis I can calculate the time complexity of radix sort and explain its dependency on the number of digits and the size of the dataset. I can calculate the time complexity of bucket sort and discuss how it depends on the number of buckets and the distribution of elements. I can compare the time complexities of radix sort, bucket sort, and comparison-based sorting methods like quicksort or mergesort. 4. General Proficiency I can identify scenarios where radix sort or bucket sort is a more efficient choice than comparison-based sorting. I feel confident in discussing the limitations of each algorithm, such as when bucket sort might struggle with uneven data distribution. I have reviewed and debugged my implementations to ensure correctness and optimal performance. 5. Reflection I have identified any areas of difficulty or confusion and sought clarification or additional resources. I feel prepared to apply these sorting algorithms in real-world applications or coursework.

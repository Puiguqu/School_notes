It's been quite a journey with eight different algorithms to understand and remember. Although we reviewed and studied what might feel like a high number of algorithms, they are not all the sorting algorithms that exist. There are many more. In the future, you might encounter some new sorting algorithm we did not study here. I expect that with the practice you have had during the last two topics, you will be able to understand and implement any new algorithm. Remember that to understand a new algorithm, you must first understand the idea behind the algorithm. That is, being able to explain it in simple words to someone else and execute it by hand with a small example. Second, recognize the main steps performed by the algorithm. Third, understand how those steps translate to pseudocode. Fourth, be able to code the algorithm from its description using pseudocode. Fifth, be able to trace your algorithm step by step to find and fix any bug in your code might have. If you've master these five steps, you will be able to master any other sorting algorithm or indeed any algorithm you might encounter in the future. It might take time depending on the difficulty of the algorithm, but you will get through it. The most important skill is not memorizing the working of specific algorithms, but understanding the main idea behind them. This will enable you to reproduce or adapt them when needed, and to understand any new algorithm you have never studied before.

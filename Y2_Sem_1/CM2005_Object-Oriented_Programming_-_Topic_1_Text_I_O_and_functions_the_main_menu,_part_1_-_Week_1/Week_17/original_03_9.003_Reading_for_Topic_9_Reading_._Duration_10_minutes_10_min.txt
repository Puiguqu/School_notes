Your Essential reading textbook is: Horton, I. and P. Van Weert Beginning CC++17: From novice to professional. (Berkeley, CA: Apress, 2018) 5th edition [9781484233665]. Remember that all the Essential reading for this programme is provided for you. Click the link which will take you to the Online Library where you can search for the books. In this topic, we are going to work intensively with member initialiser lists. You can read more about them in the textbook: Chapter 11, p.394–395. One particular technique we will use is initialising a data member by calling its constructor in the member initialiser list, as opposed to simply assigning something to it. The section 'Initializing member variables that are classes' in the following reference covers that scenario: 'Constructor member initilizer lists', Learn CPP (updated 2020) https://www.learncpp.com/cpp-tutorial/8-5a-constructor-member-initializer-lists/ accessed 17 April 2020. We also cover threads in this topic. Cross platform thread support in C++ std is somewhat limited, but luckily JUCE has a simple-to-use Thread class. The documentation for that class is here: 'Thread class reference', JUCE (no date) https://docs.juce.com/master/classThread.html accessed 17 April 2020.

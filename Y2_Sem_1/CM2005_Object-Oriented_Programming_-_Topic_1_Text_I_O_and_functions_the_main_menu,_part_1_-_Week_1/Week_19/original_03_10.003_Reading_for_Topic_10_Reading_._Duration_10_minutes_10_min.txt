In this topic, we will be focusing on a very specific set of JUCE classes that allow us to present tabular data in a JUCE application. We will encounter the 'new' keyword in this topic. This opens up the world of pointers and dynamic memory allocation, which we have not covered in depth in this course. We have been allocating memory dynamically since the std::vector class does this for us, under the hood. For further reading on pointers and dynamic memory allocation, refer to your textbook: Horton, I. and P. Van Weert Beginning C++17: From novice to professional. (Berkeley, CA: Apress, 2018) 5th edition [9781484233665]. Chapter 6, pp.196–199. Remember that all the Essential reading for this programme is provided for you.

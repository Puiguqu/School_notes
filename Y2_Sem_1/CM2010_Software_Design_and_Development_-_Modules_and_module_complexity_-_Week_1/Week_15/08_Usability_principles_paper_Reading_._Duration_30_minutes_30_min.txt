1. The text discusses the 10 Usability Principles/Heuristics for User Interface Design, first introduced by Jakob Nielsen on April 24, 1994.
2. The principles were updated on January 30, 2024, and can be found on the Web page https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ten-usability-heuristics/.
3. An archived version of the original article can also be found at https://web.archive.org/web/20250201234017/https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ten-usability-heuristics/.
4. A reference to an early Nielsen paper is available in the Online Library, titled "Improving a human-computer dialogue", published in Communications of the ACM in March 1990.
5. The text provides instructions for two activities: extracting the list of principles from the web page and evaluating a piece of software against these principles.
6. A video on Usability principles can be used as a reference for evaluation.

7. The 10 Usability Heuristics are:
   -1. Visibility of System Status
   -2. Match between System and the Real World
   -3. User Control and Freedom
   -4. Error Prevention and Recovery
   -5. Recognition rather than Recall
   -6. Flexibility and Efficiency of Use
   -7. Aesthetic and Minimalist Design
   -8. Consistency and Standards
   -9. Help Users Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors
   -10. Intuitive Footings

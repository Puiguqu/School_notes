Here's a summary of the main points:

**What is Module Cohesion?**

Module cohesion refers to the degree to which the elements within a module are related to each other and work together to achieve a common goal. It is an important concept in software design because it helps ensure that modules are modular, reusable, and maintainable.

**Types of Module Cohesion**

The speaker discusses five types of module cohesion:

1. **Temporal Cohesion**: This type of cohesion refers to the degree to which two or more elements within a module are related in time.
2. **Functional Cohesion**: This type of cohesion refers to the degree to which two or more elements within a module perform similar functions.
3. **Communicational Cohesion**: This type of cohesion refers to the degree to which two or more elements within a module communicate with each other.
4. **Procedural Cohesion**: This type of cohesion refers to the degree to which two or more elements within a module are related in terms of procedure or process.
5. **Physical Cohesion**: This type of cohesion refers to the degree to which two or more elements within a module are physically located together.

**Why Different Types of Module Cohesion are Good or Bad**

The speaker discusses why different types of module cohesion are good or bad, based on their characteristics and the benefits they provide:

* **Temporal Cohesion**: This type of cohesion is considered bad because it implies that two or more elements within a module are related in time, which may not be the case. It can also lead to tight coupling between modules.
* **Functional Cohesion**: This type of cohesion is considered good because it ensures that two or more elements within a module perform similar functions and work together to achieve a common goal.
* **Communicational Cohesion**: This type of cohesion is considered good because it ensures that two or more elements within a module communicate with each other effectively.
* **Procedural Cohesion**: This type of cohesion is considered bad because it implies that two or more elements within a module are related in terms of procedure or process, which may not be the case. It can also lead to tight coupling between modules.
* **Physical Cohesion**: This type of cohesion is considered good because it ensures that two or more elements within a module are physically located together and can share resources efficiently.

**Conclusion**

The speaker concludes by emphasizing the importance of understanding different types of module cohesion and how they relate to each other.

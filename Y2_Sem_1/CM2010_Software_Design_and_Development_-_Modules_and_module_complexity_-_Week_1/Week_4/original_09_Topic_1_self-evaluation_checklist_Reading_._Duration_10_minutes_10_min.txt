# Topic 1 self-evaluation checklist

. Duration: 10 minutes
10 min

As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Topic 1. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge. Understanding cohesion I can explain the concept of module cohesion and its importance in software design. I can identify different types of cohesion (e.g., functional, sequential, communicational) in a program. Understanding coupling I can describe the concept of module coupling and its impact on modularity. I can distinguish between different types of coupling (e.g., content, common, control, data). Analysis and categorisation I can analyse a program to determine its level of cohesion and coupling. I can assign appropriate categories of cohesion and coupling to different modules in a program. Practical application I can write programs using variables, control flow, and functions with high cohesion and low coupling. I can refactor code to improve its cohesion and reduce coupling where necessary. Critical thinking I can evaluate the trade-offs between coupling and cohesion in complex software systems. I understand how coupling and cohesion impact maintainability, scalability, and readability of code.

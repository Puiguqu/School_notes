The module consists of three micro-modules: Career Planning, Professional Impact, Profile, and Success; Global Employability Skills; and an additional module on web applications.

The Career Planning micro-module will help students construct and develop a personal career plan aligned with their preferred professional sector. The Professional Impact, Profile, and Success micro-module aims to enhance professional skills and impact. Improved skill awareness is crucial for maximizing career success.

The Global Employability Skills micro-module focuses on developing skills and describing them to future employers.

The module introduces web applications, including the three-tier architecture, databases, and HTTP protocols.

Week 1 covers basic concepts of web applications, including reading, assignments, and videos (6 minutes).

Week 2-5 focuses on building web apps with Node and Express, generating web pages from data using templates, handling forms to input data, and representing data in relational databases.

Weeks 8-11 cover basic database operations, providing access to databases from middleware. Weeks 12-13 discuss database design.

The mid-term assessment (Week 14) will test students' understanding of the material.

Advanced database operations are covered in weeks 15-16, followed by security and networking concepts in weeks 17-20.

Preparation for the exam begins in Week 21, with Exam week scheduled for Week 22.

The Careers video provides additional resources and information on web applications, databases, and career development.

References to additional reading and practice assignments are provided throughout the module.

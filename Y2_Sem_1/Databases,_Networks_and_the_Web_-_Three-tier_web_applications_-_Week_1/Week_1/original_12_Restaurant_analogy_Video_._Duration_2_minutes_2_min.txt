Imagine you were planning to open a restaurant, what would you need? First of all, you need to find place for your restaurant and you need to make it a pleasant and welcoming environment for your customers. Then you need some basic furniture and utensils, tables, chairs, dishes, cutlery, menus, and so on. You'd need a well equipped kitchen which of course needs to be staffed with chefs and supervised by Head Chef. Last but definitely not least, of course, you need fresh and good quality ingredients. You might be wondering, where am I going with this? Can you link this restaurant analogy to three-tier web architecture? Let's think this through. Which part of your restaurant is the presentation tier? The part of your restaurant that your customers would see. Of course, customers would see the section you are serving your food in, which is the dining area or a canteen. So they can see the table and chair arrangements, cutlery, plates, and cups, and so on. What about data tier?. Where is that in your restaurant? That's the kitchen and the staff working in it. The third tier, which is called the database tier, three tier web architecture is the fridge freezer where you keep and store your food ingredients. Now, can you work out which parts are on the client side and which parts are server-side? That's right. The dining area is the client side, whereas the kitchen and for the storage areas are located at the server side.

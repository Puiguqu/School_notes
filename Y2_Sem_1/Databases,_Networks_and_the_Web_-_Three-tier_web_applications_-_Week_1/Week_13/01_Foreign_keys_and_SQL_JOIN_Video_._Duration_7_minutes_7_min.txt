2. Foreign keys are used to model relations between two tables by referencing the primary key of another table.
3. The author name in the article table is an example of a foreign key, referring to the primary key author ID in the author table.
4. Author name is not a suitable primary key for the author table and foreign key for the article table due to its non-uniqueness.

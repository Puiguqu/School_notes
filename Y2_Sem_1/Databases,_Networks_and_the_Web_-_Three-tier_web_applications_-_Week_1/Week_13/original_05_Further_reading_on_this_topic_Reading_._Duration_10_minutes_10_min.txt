Further reading In this module, you have used a simple approach to ER diagrams, usually referred to as ‘crow’s feet notation’. There are more comprehensive data modelling notations, such as Chen notation or UML. You have also experienced the design and implementation of a rather simple, small, dynamic application. There are more issues involved in the development of larger and more complicated applications. When you have completed this topic, we recommend the following readings. Please note that because they are supplemental readings, we are unable to guarantee they are available in our Online Library. Elmasri and Navathe, Chapter 3: Data modelling using the Entity-Relationship (ER) model . Ceri et al. Chapter 6: Overview of the development process .

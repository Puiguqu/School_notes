So, inevitably, it's a very hands-on module with lots of lab activities. Engaging fully with the lab activities will give you the best chance of succeeding. There are 27 lab activities. By the end of the module, you should have a set of folders on your computer containing code that takes you step-by-step from a simple Hello World application to a simple but complete Bookshop application. In the first few labs, you will learn the fundamentals of Node.js, from creating a simple web server to using the Express application framework and processing web forms. In the next group of labs, you will get hands-on with relational databases using a database engine called MySQL, and a language called Structured Query Language, or SQL. ing that, you will combine Node.js and MySQL to create a dynamic, database-driven web application. SQL is a very powerful language, so you will then move on to explore some of the more advanced techniques in SQL. Finally, a really important topic of security in web applications will be addressed. So, how do you get the most out of these lab activities? First, make sure you actually do the labs. They are not optional and are an essential part of your learning. Don't just go through the steps of the lab. Getting the lab to work is not the end goal. The end goal is understanding how the lab code works and being able to apply the techniques in a new scenario. So, once you've completed the lab, take some time to understand how it works. Your real learning will come through your own exploration of the techniques used in the labs, and the techniques that exist beyond what is covered in the labs. At the end of each lab, there are some ideas for things for you to explore. But don't just stop there. See what else you can discover through your own research. Finally, after many of the labs, I've recorded a post-lab video where I deconstruct what you did in the labs to help your understanding, and sometimes explore a bit further. Do watch these.

This second module marks the continuation of the "Introduction to Programming" adventure using JavaScript and p5.js. The module is designed to build upon the core programming skills acquired in the initial part. Students will explore larger code bases and bigger projects, transitioning from basic coding to advanced implementation. The main focus will be on object-oriented programming (OOP) to help write larger and more complex programs. Specifically, the course will delve into advanced OOP concepts and utilizing coding techniques learned previously. The curriculum will not present much new syntax, but rather focus on the practical application of existing skills. The learning process emphasizes how to organize, plan, and evaluate code effectively. Three major case studies will center the practical demonstrations and descriptions. These case studies are a data visualizer, a drawing app, and a music visualizer. Students will be assigned the task of selecting one case study and extending its functionality with their own custom code. Beyond the case studies, the course structure includes professional discussions and debugging challenges. These challenges aim to hone skills through real-world, fieldish debugging scenarios. The curriculum begins by reviewing foundational object-oriented techniques and code organization principles. The accompanying syllabus outlines a comprehensive 21-week learning path. Early weeks focus heavily on revisiting core object concepts and practicing OOP in detail. Following foundational review, the module systematically introduces the three case studies over several weeks. Weeks 3 and 4 are dedicated to the "Drawing App" case study development. Weeks 5 and 6 focus on the "Music Visualiser" case study. Weeks 7 and 8 are reserved for the "Data Visualisation" case study. Subsequent weeks involve expanding upon and refining these applications. Topics include advanced development stages, callbacks, and stability testing. The final phase covers specialized topics such as testing with users, culminating in the final Project and Report assignment. Participants are expected to find the content challenging yet highly rewarding for understanding practical programming implications.

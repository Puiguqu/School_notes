1. The module begins with a warm welcome, encouraging students to get started by familiarizing themselves with the tasks and structure of the forthcoming weeks.
2. It is advised that students read the module syllabus thoroughly to gain a comprehensive understanding of the content.
3. Viewing the module introduction video is highly recommended to learn about the overall scope and objectives of the module.
4. Students must review their learning outcomes, which are summarized in the syllabus, detailing the core knowledge and skills expected upon completion.
5. Furthermore, checking the assessment section of the module syllabus is necessary to understand how the module can be passed.
6. For those taking their first module or wishing to deepen their knowledge of the Coursera platform, the Orientation course should be utilized.
7. The program necessitates the use of three crucial communication tools: discussion forums, Slack, and Zoom.
8. Details regarding these three important communication tools can be found through the assigned Orientation course material.
9. Students are instructed to introduce themselves to their classmates in the dedicated discussion forum titled ‘Getting to know each other.’
10. Before participating in the forums, students should review the essential guidelines provided in the Orientation course to ensure proper usage.
11. Specific information concerning live webinars is also available for review within the Orientation course material.
12. The Online Library offers an extensive amount of information and resources designed to support academic studies.
13. In the Orientation course, students will also be introduced to Studiosity, a new online service the University is piloting for academic writing support.
14. This unique resource provides additional help and refreshers for various topics encountered during the studies.
15. The University of London Careers Service offers comprehensive support regardless of a student's current career stage.
16. Support is provided for 'career starters' who are beginning their professional journey.
17. The service also assists 'career developers' who already possess work-based experience.
18. Additionally, 'career changers' looking to transition their professional fields are fully supported by the service.
19. Students can read more about enhancing career prospects and the full scope of the University of London’s Career Service on the Orientation course.
20. Aligning academic study directly with personal career goals is crucial for maintaining motivation and staying on track for success.
21. Initiating career alignment can be done by examining the Careers Connect platform.
22. Students are encouraged to attend the live, targeted BSc Computer Science careers webinars scheduled throughout the entire programme.
23. The resources for rebooting job hunting include an overview of the University of London Careers Service YouTube channel, Micro-Modules, and the Vacancies Board.
24. Access to all these essential career resources is managed through the Careers Connect platform.
25. The module team concludes by extending best wishes and expressing anticipation for the students’ contributions as the program progresses.

The comprehensive curriculum details a 21-week project structure focused on advanced application development and software testing. The initial weeks introduce foundational concepts, starting with Object Orientation in Practice across Weeks 1 and 2. The subsequent weeks transition into hands-on case studies, beginning with the drawing app in Weeks 3 and 4. Following this, the focus shifts to the music visualiser in Weeks 5 and 6. Weeks 7 and 8 introduce the third major case study: data visualisation.

A significant phase begins in Week 9 with the topic of "Extending the apps, part 1," continuing through Week 10. This extension process requires the students to develop more complex application extensions, which are detailed further in Weeks 11 and 12. Technical learning objectives are highly specific, including extending the drawing app to feature editable shapes (a 26-minute lesson). For the music visualiser, students learn to incorporate blocks for mid, high, and low frequencies.

Specific data visualisation techniques are taught in depth, covering everything from gathering and collating survey data to sketching and finally building waffle charts, the latter session running for 55 minutes. The project continues with "Extending the apps, part 2" in Week 12, followed by "Extending the apps, part 3" across Weeks 13 and 14. A key technical concept introduced during this period is the study of Callbacks, spanning Weeks 15 and 16.

The latter third of the program concentrates heavily on quality assurance and development integrity. Weeks 17 and 18 address the vital process of Testing for stability, ensuring the robustness of the applications. The curriculum then moves into user-centric development in Weeks 19 and 20, focusing on Testing with users.

Beyond the technical modules, the project structure emphasizes academic accountability and planning. The program requires the development of a detailed plan for time management between the present date and the final deadline. Students must complete a midterm report, which serves as a critical opportunity to receive expert feedback and confirm the sufficiency of their initial project ideas. The module content notes that the second set of application extensions are inherently more complex than previous iterations, requiring detailed design and planning. Furthermore, the course integrates into a supportive academic environment, including guided project progress checks, student interviews, and the final Project and report assignment in Week 21.

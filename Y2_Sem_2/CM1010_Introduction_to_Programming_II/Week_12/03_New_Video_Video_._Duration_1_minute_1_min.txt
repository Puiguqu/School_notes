This summary addresses the usage of the Studiosity service, details technical navigation methods, and outlines a comprehensive academic schedule.

Studiosity is an essential service designed to help students boost confidence and achieve their academic best this year. It is highly recommended that students integrate using this service into their general study routine. To access the service, students must first log into their institution's student portal. The Studiosity link may be located on the Portal Homepage, the course page, or within a study support services menu. If the required link cannot be found, students are advised to contact support services for assistance. Once logged in, it is crucial for the user to bookmark the page for quick future access. Studiosity offers two primary services for student support. The first option provides personalized, same-day feedback on any written draft. Users should click "submit your document" and follow the provided prompts to utilize this feature. Feedback is given on crucial aspects including structure, language, spelling, and grammar. A common practice is uploading drafts before going to bed to receive feedback the following morning. Reviewing the feedback is confirmed via both email and mobile notification to the student. Students can then make necessary revisions before the final work submission. The second service is instant support via live chat with a specialist. To connect, one must click "connect now" to link with the next available expert. These specialists can help with various subjects, including writing, English skills and concepts, math, and science. Furthermore, the service provides essential research tips and guidance on staying on the right academic track. On the technical level, navigating the transcript can be managed using the 'tab' key. To save a specific note, one can use the combination CTRL + S. For expanding a selection, the sequence CTRL + arrow key is used, while contracting selection requires shift + CTRL + arrow key. Screen readers incompatible with arrow key shortcuts can replace them with the H J K L keys, and some require using CTRL alongside the alt key. The academic schedule spans 21 weeks, covering key topics like 'Object orientation in practice' and 'data visualisation.' Significant modules include 'Introducing case study 2: music visualiser' in Week 5, and 'Callbacks' in Week 15. The schedule culminates in a final 'Project and report assignment' in Week 21. Specific assessments include the 'Interim project progress [001]', which is a Graded Assignment lasting 2 hours. Other required activities involve a 'What is Studiosity?' Reading (10 minutes) and an 'Studiosity' Ungraded App Item (1 hour). The schedule details specific timed components like a 20-minute 'Discussion Prompt' and a 10-minute 'What does the feedback cover' Reading.

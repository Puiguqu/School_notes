This course requires students to be actively preparing their final midterm submission while learning about advanced extensions in the current module. The overarching goal of this topic is for students to make definitive decisions regarding their final project selection. These extensions represent the most complex material presented, designed to test cumulative knowledge built across the previous one and a half modules. The standard of complexity observed in these extensions defines the level required to achieve excellent marks on the final project. It is highly recommended that students review every extension covered in this topic, even if the project is not intended to utilize that specific technique. Reviewing all extensions ensures exposure to a broader range of creative ideas and technical implementations. The curriculum will introduce several practical brainstorming techniques to help students begin scoping their ambitious projects effectively. Furthermore, the course structure includes a dedicated final student interview component to guide project development. Early foundational topics included Week 1's Object Orientation in Practice, which continued through Week 2. Subsequent case studies covered Weeks 3 and 4 with the Drawing App, and Weeks 5 and 6 introduced the Music Visualiser. Weeks 7 and 8 focused on Data Visualisation, establishing three major projects. The core content then progressed through extension modules, beginning in Week 9 (Part 1). Weeks 10 and 11 covered further extensions, followed by Week 12 (Part 2) and Week 13 (Part 3). Technical development continued in Week 14, concluding the primary extension material. Later, Week 15 and 16 covered the crucial technical topic of Callbacks, which was further explored in Week 17 and Week 18. Practical skills escalated with Week 19 and Week 20, focusing on Testing for Stability and Testing with Users.

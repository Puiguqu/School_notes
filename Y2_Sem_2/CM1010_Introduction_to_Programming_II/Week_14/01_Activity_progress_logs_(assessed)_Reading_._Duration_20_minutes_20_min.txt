# Activity – progress logs (assessed)

The primary purpose of the project log is fundamentally important for successful academic progression. This log system helps students monitor what they have successfully achieved in their project. Furthermore, maintaining these logs will keep the project on track at all times. Crucially, the logs also allow instructors to assess how the student has approached the project development. They provide evidence of how the student tackled a larger application framework. Students are required to complete a log in the second half of every assigned topic. These logs are mandatory and must be completed for each distinct topic area. The final collection of logs must be submitted along with the project's final submission. The structure for these logs has been provided via a dedicated template. Specific file formats are available to facilitate log creation. These formats include Rich Text Format (RTF). The RTF option is titled: `Introduction to Programming II project log RTF File`. Additionally, a Microsoft Word document format is provided. This word format is labeled: `Introduction to Programming II project log DOCX File`. Students must ensure they utilize the template to properly structure their record of progress. Each log entry requires the student to clearly state their overall project title. The log must also identify the specific current topic under discussion. A key section requires detailing the progress made on the topic at hand. Students must report on any problems they encountered during the work period. It is essential to include whether or not those identified problems were ultimately solved. Furthermore, the log requires a planning statement regarding the next few weeks of work. Students must confirm if they are currently on target for successful completion of the project. If the student determines they are not on target, they must outline a clear plan to address the issue. All these completed progress logs must be compiled into one single submission file. Students must take care to keep all the completed logs safe on their local computer system. The total requirement is that students must complete and submit four separate progress logs. After all logs are finalized, the entire document must be converted. The final, consolidated submission format must be a Portable Document Format (PDF).

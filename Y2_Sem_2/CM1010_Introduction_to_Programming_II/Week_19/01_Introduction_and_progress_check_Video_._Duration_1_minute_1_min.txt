This module segment concludes the instruction, marking the final topics from Edward Deming before students begin their projects. Currently, students should possess a firm understanding of the remaining requirements needed for project completion. While students may not be at the absolute finishing stage, substantial coding and planned extensions must be in place. Furthermore, students are advised to proactively utilize the testing techniques introduced in the preceding topic. Although a complete test plan is not expected, developing detailed tests for the more complex elements of the project is highly recommended. This final topic introduces essential principles governing usability testing. These principles focus on ensuring that the developed application successfully meets the needs of its intended users. Simultaneously, students must begin drafting the accompanying report, which must be a concise piece of technical writing. To ensure quality, drafting the report using a word processor or text editor with functional spellcheck is strongly advised. This allows the production of detailed and clear professional writing.

The course curriculum is highly structured across 21 weeks, detailing specific technical concepts. Early weeks covered foundational topics like Object Orientation in Practice (Weeks 1-2). Subsequent weeks introduced major case studies, including the drawing app (Week 3-4), the music visualiser (Week 5-6), and data visualisation (Week 7-8). Progress continued through practical development, focusing on extending the apps over several weeks (Weeks 9-14). Core technical concepts were then covered in depth, addressing Callbacks over two weeks (Weeks 15-16). The curriculum advanced into stability testing (Weeks 17-18).

The latter half of the course focuses heavily on testing methodologies and user interaction. Week 19 dedicated material to general testing with users, followed by Week 20 continuing this specialized topic. The module structure for advanced topics, such as Lesson 10.2, includes timed components. For example, Usability testing is detailed over seven minutes, while the subsequent writing the report component lasts five minutes. Students are assigned a 30-minute Usability testing Practice Assignment and a 20-minute Discussion Prompt concerning usability testing.

Technical navigation details for the video platform are also provided for reference. General transcript navigation can be handled using the Tab key. To save a note, the shortcut is CTRL + S. Expanding a selection requires CTRL + arrow key, while contracting a selection uses Shift + CTRL + arrow key. For screen readers incompatible with these arrow key shortcuts, alternative keys are H, J, K, and L. Furthermore, some screen readers may mandate using CTRL in conjunction with the Alt key. The video platform also offers a "Theater mode" for an immersive experience with dark mode and an auto-scrolling transcript.

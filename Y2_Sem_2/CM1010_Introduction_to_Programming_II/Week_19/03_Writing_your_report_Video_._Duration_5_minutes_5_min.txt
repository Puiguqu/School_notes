The final project submission requires a comprehensive short report alongside the code, which must cover the project's technical achievements, implementation efficacy, and critical process evaluation. A strong report is critical for achieving high marks, regardless of the coding quality alone. If diagrams or images are included, they should be placed in the submission folder and referenced, rather than linking to external cloud hosting sites. The report is structured around four key areas of questioning.

For the first section, students must list all modifications and extensions made to the provided templates, adhering to a word limit of approximately 400 words. For each extension, detailed inclusion is required, specifying its functionality, how it integrated into the template's design, and the underlying code structure. Marketers will evaluate the technical implementation, requiring analysis of techniques used and the effect of object-oriented principles (OOP) on the final build.

The second question demands an evaluation of the plan's effectiveness, requiring a review of the original midterm GANTT chart. Students must address whether they remained on schedule, detail any deviations, and assess the effectiveness of task granularity. This section requires reflection on unexpected difficulties, pinpointing specific code segments that took longer than anticipated and how those challenges were ultimately resolved.

The third section mandates a critical evaluation of the final product as a whole, promoting self-reflection on the entire process. It is crucial to reflect on potential changes, rather than simply assessing personal coding ability. Furthermore, the student must consider the process of user or system testing, noting how these tests helped uncover errors and how different users responded to the application. Future versions should also be scoped, suggesting additions, removals, or changes.

The fourth and final section is an opportunity to list all external sources used in the project for complete attribution. This must be highly specific, listing projects or questions—not just general sites like Stack Overflow or GitHub. Students must provide links for any borrowed code, inspiration, pseudocode, or converted code from other languages. Additionally, all third-party libraries utilized and any AI prompts used, along with their responses, must be explicitly included.

While using external sources is necessary, care must be taken not to overuse third-party libraries outside of P5Js, as this detracts from demonstrating the student's original coding skills. Students are also advised to include the required project logs compiled throughout the project duration. Finally, the report must maintain correct English spelling and grammar, be well-structured using clean points and paragraphs, and adopt an appropriate academic tone throughout the document.

In this video, I'm going to give you more detail about what to include in the report section of your final project submission. Alongside your code submission, we also want you to write a short report about how your project has gone. And this will be divided up into several sections that you'll be able to input on the Coursera platform. In your report, we are looking for a technical summary of what you've achieved, how effectively your plan was implemented, and a critical evaluation of the process you went through. Very often the key to getting a great mark on this module is to write a good report. There are many examples of students who did a good piece of coding but then didn't spend enough time on the final report or and then ended up with a mark below their expectations. One thing to note is that if you're struggling to include images or diagrams in your submission. Then you'll be able to add them to your submission folder and just reference them or point to them in the report. I would recommend doing this instead of linking to online cloud hosting sites. The report sections consist of four questions. For the first, you need to list any modifications and extensions that you have made to the templates. We give a word limit of around 400 words for this. For each extension that you've produced, you should include what its functionality is, how you fitted that into the template's design, and how you structured the code. To get great marks on this, you should be thinking carefully about the technical implementation, not just the nature of the extension itself. What techniques did you use and how did you do it? How did you make use of object orientation? How did that affect the final implementation? In the second question, we'd like you to describe how effective your plan was for completing the project. Thinking back to your midterm and the GANTT chart that you made, you should include whether you stuck to the schedule. If you didn't, where did you deviate from this plan? Did you divide up your time effectively? Did you choose the right granularity of tasks to include in your GANTT chart? Did you come across any challenges or unexpected difficulties? What were the bits of your code that took longer than you expected and how did you overcome those challenges? Many of the markers will go back to your midterm when reviewing this question and they'll look at what you planned and how effective it was with regard to your final output. For the third question, we want you to perform an evaluation of your project. How effective is it as a final product? You should include some self evaluation here of how effectively you completed the project. Process Again, go back to your original project plan and think what you might do differently next time. But be warned, we're not looking for you to reflect on how good you are at code, but rather how effectively you can think about the overall process and how you worked through it. You also need to consider whether you have performed any user or system testing on your application. Was this part of your process? Did it help to uncover errors? How did different users respond to your application, and what might you do in a future version? What things might you add, remove or change? The final question number four isn't so much a question, but more an opportunity for you to list any external sources that you've used in your project. For example, you may have taken some code from somewhere else and included that within your project, or you might have taken some code and used it as inspiration. Perhaps you found some pseudocode for an algorithm, or you found code in another language that you converted to JavaScript. You might have also looked at online help, for example Stack Overflow or library documentation. Make sure you list exactly what you've used here. Be specific. Don't just put down stack overflow or GitHub. Provide links to the particular projects or questions that you've used. You should also list any third party libraries that you've used in your project. We also want you to include any AI prompts that you may have used and the responses that you have received from that system. However, don't forget that we try and discourage you from using third party libraries outside of P5Js. It can have a negative effect on your work. We want you to demonstrate your coding skills rather than the implementation of a particular library. Some libraries take away your ability to be creative and to produce work that you've really built from scratch. We'd rather see an end result that perhaps doesn't look as aesthetically pleasing, but is a better demonstration of your coding abilities. Please don't think, though, that you need to include everything you've ever read or that has helped you. This can be a revised version of the answer that you provided in the midterm. Finally, I want to talk a little bit about the quality of these reports. Obviously, we're looking for correct English spelling and grammar here. We also want to think about how readable your work is. Your report should be well structured. This might mean simple things like paragraphs, but also making sure you provide clean, clear points. You should use the correct tone and vocabulary for academic work. You should also include figures or images that help to make your argument.

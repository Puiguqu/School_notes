Peer review is defined as a collaborative academic process where students, scholars, or researchers evaluate and critique each other’s work to facilitate mutual learning. In an academic setting, this involves carefully reading and critiquing a peer’s assignment's content and arguments. For Computer Science programs, peer reviews often form part of required assignments. These peer-graded assignments have two mandatory components. First, the student must submit their own assignment for grading by peers. Second, the student must grade a minimum number of peers' submitted assignments to receive a grade for their own work. When grading, participants receive a rubric, which describes assignment components and the response types corresponding to each possible grade. Rubrics are essential tools used to ensure consistency in grading and help learners understand academic expectations. The core purpose of peer review transcends mere critique; its goal is to offer constructive feedback that aids the author's improvement. This process is fundamentally valuable because it forces engagement with how others understand the same question addressed. Consequently, it is crucial for developing critical thinking, fostering collaborative learning, and upholding academic integrity. A primary benefit is the enhancement of critical thinking and analytical skills, as evaluating others’ work sharpens one’s own analytical abilities. By identifying strengths, weaknesses, and gaps in a peer’s writing, participants develop a keen eye for detail. Evaluating peers’ research, structure, and writing style also improves one's own writing and research proficiency. Furthermore, peer review exposes participants to diverse viewpoints and methodologies, offering new perspectives and ideas. This exposure can stimulate creativity and broaden understanding across subjects. The process inherently fosters a sense of community and collaboration among participants, developing supportive academic networks. Critically, peer review prepares individuals for future professional and academic endeavors. This simulated process mirrors scholarly review practices, preparing students for intellectual discourse and revision. Moreover, teaching or critiquing another’s work is highly effective for learning and retention, reinforcing one's own knowledge and mastery. Another key skill developed is empathy and communication, requiring the delivery of constructive, respectful, and positive feedback. Engaging in reviews also provides insight into the formal academic review process, demystifying scholarly publishing. Participants gain increased exposure to diverse research topics and methodologies, which can inspire interdisciplinary study. Finally, successfully engaging in peer review boosts academic confidence by providing a tangible sense of accomplishment and assurance in one's own skills. (25 sentences)

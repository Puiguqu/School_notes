# Reflection on interview with the student
Reading•
. Duration: 30 minutes
30 min - Summary

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-introduction-to-programming-2/supplement/O9Lwq/reflection-on-interview-with-the-student

After watching the interview with Taylor, students must thoroughly reflect on the progress of their personal project. When writing notes on the interview, consideration must be given to the student's own design improvements. A key inquiry is whether Taylor's completed application features inspire new functionality for the student’s drawing application, or any other project. Students should evaluate whether they wish to redesign the core application functionality, for instance, by adding advanced controls to the music visualizer. The interview specifically highlighted different methods for manipulating the waveform, prompting students to generate similar novel manipulation ideas. Furthermore, the technical scope requires considering the integration of peripheral devices like the camera or the microphone into the application. A critical planning element is detailing how to handle the technical failure or unavailability of these required devices. For the midterm assignment, the student must detail a robust implementation plan to maximize its overall benefit. Identifying appropriate planning techniques is essential to successfully support the implementation of more advanced features. Brainstorming various designs and continuously iterating through different concepts can ultimately result in a superior project outcome. To manage the project, students must formulate a clear assessment strategy for tracking current progress. Determining whether they are on target requires establishing metrics to monitor timely completion and required standard adherence. Regarding evaluation, the student must replicate Taylor’s process of reflecting on project achievements and shortcomings. This final reflection process is mandatory upon submission of the final application. To facilitate this process, students should immediately begin consistently tracking their thinking and operational progress. Specifically, they need to keep a detailed record of brainstorming designs and initial sketches. Identifying sources of inspiration and technical assistance must also be meticulously documented. Finally, a regular logging of all project progress is necessary for comprehensive record-keeping. These accumulated records—sketches, sources, and progress logs—are critical assessment components. All these materials will be required for upload during both the midterm and end-of-term assessments. Therefore, proactive documentation is vital to support the overall development lifecycle. The student must approach this self-reflection process with deep analytical thought. This detailed approach ensures comprehensive coverage of all project facets. It helps define actionable next steps for the remaining development phase. This systematic process guarantees adherence to academic project requirements.

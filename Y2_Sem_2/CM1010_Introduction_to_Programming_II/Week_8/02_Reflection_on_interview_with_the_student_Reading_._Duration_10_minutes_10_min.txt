# Reflection on interview with the student

. Duration: 10 minutes

Following the viewing of Simas's interview, dedicated reflection on one's own project progress is highly recommended. When documenting notes from the interview, one must consider multiple dimensions of the personal project development. The initial focus area is the project’s design and potential feature enhancements. Students should ask if Simas’s implemented features inspire modifications to their own application. Specifically, consider redesigning core application functionality is a key element of this reflection. This includes making changes to initial data visualizations and adding supplementary interactivity. Simas demonstrated that animation serves as an effective tool within data visualization practices. Therefore, students must determine how to incorporate dynamic animation into their own designs. Furthermore, the technical challenge of implementing complex object-oriented structures must be addressed. Simas utilized specific techniques, such as constructor functions, which illustrate these structures. The next major phase of reflection centers on the detailed implementation planning for the midterm assignment. Students must articulate how to maximize the benefit of this comprehensive planning process. Critical planning techniques are needed to support the integration of more advanced features into the system. Brainstorming various design iterations and rigorously engaging in design changes is paramount. This iterative approach is essential for achieving a demonstrably superior final project outcome. Students must establish clear methods for assessing their current project progress objectively. A defined metric is required to ensure timely completion that meets the required academic standard. The final major area of reflection involves the evaluation phase, mirroring Simas’s discussion of things he would change. Students will similarly be asked to reflect on their achievements and deficiencies upon final submission. It is highly advised to begin tracking thinking and progress notes immediately to facilitate this comprehensive process. Specific items to diligently track include detailed brainstorming designs and foundational sketches. Sources of inspiration and technical assistance must also be systematically documented for future review. A regular and thorough logging of project progress is arguably the most crucial habit to develop now. These accumulated records will be required for inclusion in both the midterm and the end-of-term assessments. Mastering this reflective process demonstrates metacognitive awareness of the project life cycle. The analysis of existing work is as valuable as the implementation of new features. By integrating these three areas—Design, Planning, and Evaluation—the project can achieve maximum depth. These considerations guide the student toward a comprehensive understanding of applied software engineering principles.

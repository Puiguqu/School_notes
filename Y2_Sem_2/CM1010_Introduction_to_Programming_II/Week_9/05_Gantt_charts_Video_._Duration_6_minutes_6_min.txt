# Gantt charts

A Gantt chart is defined as an organized list of tasks, their timing, and their interrelationships, providing a visual display of project progress. This tool assists teams by determining necessary resources and mapping current project progress. Structurally, a Gantt chart comprises a task list detailing required actions, a timeline section, and bars that indicate the degree of completion or crucial milestones. For simplicity, the recommended tool for constructing this chart is Microsoft Excel, utilizing a provided template available on the Coursera platform. The setup requires populating the left column with project tasks and the upper columns with remaining weeks, such as Week 9 through Week 20. Specific milestones, like the midterm and final submission, must be identified, guiding the placement of tasks around these fixed deadlines. When constructing the chart, careful consideration must be given to the granularity of tasks, which refers to the level of required detail. Tasks can range from low granularity (e.g., "write reports") to higher granularity (e.g., "code update function" or "write the project summary"). The recommended approach is to balance the desired detail level while ensuring tasks are clear, concise, and realistically manageable. The planning process involves allocating dedicated time slots to each task, visually represented by color-coded filled cells within the timeline. For instance, a student might dedicate three days in Week 9 for "Brainstorming ideas for extensions" and subsequent time for "Code update function for extension 1." Beyond the general structure, the module follows a defined curricular path from Week 1 to Week 21. Early weeks introduce core concepts, covering "Object orientation in practice" (Weeks 1 and 2). Weeks 3 through 8 are dedicated to introducing and continuing three distinct case studies: the drawing app (Case Study 1), the music visualiser (Case Study 2), and data visualisation (Case Study 3). Lesson 5.1 (Week 9) initiates project planning, covering topics like "The use of AI in your project work" and generating the Gantt chart. Subsequently, Lesson 5.2 continues project development, focusing on extending the drawing app with a "stamp tool" and extending the data visualizer with "existing data sources." The project timeline continues through Weeks 10-14, advancing through various extensions, before tackling specific concepts in Weeks 15-17, such as "Callbacks" and "Testing for stability." Students must understand that the Gantt chart is not a static object, but a dynamic document requiring constant updates and tracking. The final report must demonstrate not only the completed chart but also a thorough justification of choices made. Critically, the final written submission must discuss whether the original time scales were met, or if they required adjustment during the project lifecycle.

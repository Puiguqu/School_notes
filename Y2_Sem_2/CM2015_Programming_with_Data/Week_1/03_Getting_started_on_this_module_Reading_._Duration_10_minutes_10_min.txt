1. The module team offers a very warm welcome to all students starting this module.
2. Students are advised to familiarize themselves with the module's structure and the tasks for the coming weeks.
3. A critical initial step is reviewing the module syllabus thoroughly.
4. Participants should watch the module introduction video to gain a comprehensive understanding of the module's scope.
5. Reviewing the learning outcomes, detailed within the syllabus, provides a summary of the required knowledge and skills.
6. The assessment section of the syllabus must be reviewed to understand the methods of passing the module.
7. For those new to the Coursera platform, or those wanting to deepen their understanding, the Orientation course is recommended.
8. Throughout the program, three essential communication tools will be used: discussion forums, Slack, and Zoom.
9. Detailed guidance on these three tools can be found within the dedicated Orientation course.
10. Students are encouraged to initiate contact with classmates by saying 'hello' in the discussion forum titled ‘Applications of Python for data programming’.
11. Before actively participating in forums, it is essential to review the usage guidelines and follow the stated essential rules found in the Orientation course.
12. The Orientation course also provides further details regarding live webinars.
13. Information about the Online Library is also available through the Orientation course.
14. The Online Library tutorial is highlighted as a valuable resource providing extensive information to aid studies.
15. Additionally, students will be introduced to Studiosity during the Orientation course.
16. Studiosity is a new online service currently being piloted by the University to support academic writing.
17. This resource offers additional help and refresher material for various academic topics encountered during studies.
18. The University of London Careers Service supports all students regardless of their career stage.
19. Career options are addressed for 'career starters,' those who are 'career developers' with work-based experience, and 'career changers.'
20. Further reading regarding career enhancement and the comprehensive services provided by the University of London Careers Service is available on the Orientation course.
21. Aligning academic study with personal career goals is strongly recommended to maintain motivation and trajectory.
22. Students can immediately start exploring career opportunities using the Careers Connect platform.
23. Attendees are encouraged to participate in the live targeted BSc Computer Science careers webinars scheduled throughout the program.
24. A video overview details the University of London’s Careers & Employability Regional Job Hunting Resources, accessible via Careers Connect.
25. The module team wishes the students the best and looks forward to their contributions throughout the module.

Data preparation is an essential and time-intensive phase in data analysis and modeling. This process is necessary because real-world data, whether stored in files or databases, is often not in the correct format for a specific task. The core tasks that constitute data preparation include loading, cleaning, transforming, and rearranging data. These preparatory tasks are frequently reported by analysts to consume 80% or even more of their total working time. To manage this data manipulation, researchers often utilize general-purpose programming languages such as Python or C. This curriculum specifically focuses on using Pandas, a library that, when integrated with Python’s built-in features, provides a flexible, high-level, and fast set of tools. The techniques taught cover methods for extracting data from unprocessed formats. A major focus is placed on organizing data into correct and specified data types. Furthermore, the course addresses the critical need for handling both missing values and duplicate entries within datasets. Analytical transformations are also key, including specific techniques to effectively filter and manage outliers in the data.

The overall curriculum structure progresses methodically across multiple weeks. The initial weeks establish the foundation, beginning with Week 1 (Introduction to the course) and Week 2 (Getting to grips with Python). Core programming concepts are built in Weeks 3 and 4, covering Python basics like data types, control flow, functions, and programming with lists. Subsequent weeks deepen the knowledge of data structures, pattern matching with strings, and file system interaction. Weeks 7 and 8 focus heavily on the practical skills of reading and writing data to and from the file system. Data acquisition methods are expanded in Week 9 and 10 with processing text data and language processing techniques, respectively.

The curriculum then progresses to advanced data retrieval in Weeks 11 and 12, focusing on retrieving data from the web. Weeks 13 and 14 are dedicated to mastering the retrieval of data from databases. Weeks 15 through 16 are highly specialized, beginning with Lesson 15.1 (Introduction to data cleaning) and Lesson 15.2 (Data transformation). These sections detail specific practices, including examining errors and managing duplicates. Lesson 15.3 focuses entirely on handling missing data and legacy data. Following this, Week 17 introduces advanced data analysis and visualization principles. The learning continues with Week 18, focusing on exploratory analysis for language processing, and Week 19, which covers advanced topics in Python and data programming. The course concludes with Week 20 (Advanced feature extraction), Week 21 (Revision), and the final assessment in Week 22 (Exam).

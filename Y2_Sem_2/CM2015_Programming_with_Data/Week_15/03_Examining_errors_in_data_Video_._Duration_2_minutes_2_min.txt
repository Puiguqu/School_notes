The analysis process begins by loading data from a CSV file, utilizing the pandas library, and subsequently storing the data within a pandas DataFrame, conventionally assigned to the variable `df`. The primary goal of the data analysis is to identify and address two major categories of data integrity issues: content-based errors and format-based errors. Initial data exploration reveals that the DataFrame contains a product and item table, requiring systematic review column by column. A critical initial step involves preprocessing the 'name' column (and potentially others) by removing any preceding tags, ensuring only the pure content remains for accurate analysis. Furthermore, it is necessary to identify and handle repeated entries, such as the product names 'Hats' and 'product1', which appear multiple times, necessitating deduplication techniques.

Regarding specific data anomalies, the 'price' column is analyzed for missing values, which introduce `NaN` entries into the dataset. Outliers are observed in the price data, including prices that are abnormally large or extremely small, all of which require specialized handling before any statistical analysis can be performed. Similarly, the 'date' column exhibits missing values and contains date outliers, such as launch dates of 3000 or 1800, which fall far outside the typical data range.

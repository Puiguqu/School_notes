The curriculum structure begins with Week 1, covering the Introduction to the course, followed by Week 2, which is titled "Getting to grips with Python." Week 2 involves structured learning modules such as "Running Jupyter notebooks locally," "Packages and virtual environments," and "Working with strings." Specific technical components addressed in Week 2 include installing Jupyter notebooks (Mac/Windows) and Conda. Week 3 tackles "Python basics: Data types, control flow, and functions," while Week 4 focuses on "Writing programs using lists and control flow." Subsequent weeks build upon data handling skills. Week 5 covers "Data structures," and Week 6 focuses on "Pattern matching with strings."

Weeks 7 and 8 are dedicated to "Reading and writing data on the file system," with Week 8 serving as Part 2. The focus then shifts to data processing in Week 9, "Processing text data," and Week 10, "Language processing." Data acquisition techniques are covered in Weeks 11 and 12, both dedicated to "Retrieving data from the web." Weeks 13 and 14 focus on "Retrieving data from the databases." Following data retrieval, Week 15 addresses "Data cleaning," and Week 16 handles "More data transformations."

The latter half of the course intensifies data analysis skills. Week 17 covers "Data analysis and visualisation," while Week 18 focuses on "Exploratory analysis for language processing." Weeks 19 and 20 cover "Advanced topics in Python and data programming" and "Advanced feature extraction," respectively. The journey concludes with Week 21 for comprehensive revision, culminating in the Week 22 Exam.

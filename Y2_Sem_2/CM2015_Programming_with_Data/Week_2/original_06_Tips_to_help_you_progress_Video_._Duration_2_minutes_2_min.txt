The video tutorials take you through step-by-step process of programming a certain concept. Don't hesitate to pause, slow them down, and watch them and code along with these video tutorials. In addition to the videos, there are several Python documentation that's also available. Documentation in Python, in addition to containing the programming concept, also has demonstrations as code snippets. In order to get a hands-on feel for what a line of code does, take a line of code, paste it in your Jupyter Notebook, and press Shift-Enter. That would help you give a quick understanding of what that line of code does. In several chapters, you will find reading materials that are designed to complement your learning along with programming. Sometimes these reading materials are about understanding the concept. Sometimes they are about understanding the syntax and writing correct code. So alongside the code, also look at the reading materials to deepen your understanding of the topic. If you ever get stuck, always use the forum. Forums tend to be a great place for interaction between students and their tutors. A quick guideline for something that you post in the forum, always provide a context on what you're stuck at. If you're stuck at a specific programming activity, maybe describe what the programming activity is along with a snippet of the code that seems to be causing you problems. Reach out to your tutor and get help as there is help to be offered. Finally, several programming activities are designed to have peer feedback. Engaging in meaningful peer feedback is a great way to learn. While you are providing feedback to your peers, ensure that you're always respectful and provide constructive feedback that help your peer progress on their learning journey.

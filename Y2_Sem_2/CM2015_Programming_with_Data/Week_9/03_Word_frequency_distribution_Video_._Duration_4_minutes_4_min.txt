# Word frequency distribution

The core objective is to analyze text content by assessing the frequency distribution of different words. This process relies heavily on utilizing word frequency distribution in conjunction with various text preprocessing techniques. Specifically, word frequency distribution is applied at the end of every preprocessing step to validate its successful execution. Key preprocessing stages examined include removing stop words, stemming, and lemmatization. The central tool for this analysis is the `NLTK.FreqDist()` function, which calculates the frequency of items. When a string is passed to the function, it analyzes individual characters, including alphabets, spaces, and punctuation. For example, in a string, 'a' might occur 3 times, while 'b' occurs 2 times. To generate a word frequency distribution, the initial string must first be split into a list of values using space as the delimiter. Passing this resultant list to `FreqDist` accurately calculates the frequency of unique words within the text. The frequency distribution object can be visually represented using the `.plot()` method, such as when calling `ft.plot()`. In this plot, the x-axis displays the different words, and the y-axis represents their respective frequency of occurrence. For instance, a uniformly distributed set of words, each occurring once, results in a straight line graph. If a specific word, like "hello," appears multiple times, the graph will show a clear peak at that word's frequency count. These techniques allow for merging repetitive word occurrences, thereby enhancing the reliability of the frequency distribution. Preprocessing steps like lemmatization or removing punctuation, such as a comma, contribute to merging these words and improving keyword frequency. Understanding this distribution enables the inference of more meaningful information from the text based on keyword frequency.

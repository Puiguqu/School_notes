Below, I’ll highlight the common mistakes and challenges encountered while pre-processing text in Python. Use this to help you review your code from the previous lab and debug any challenges you may have had. 1. Improper tokenization: Mistake: Not handling punctuation correctly during tokenization, leading to tokens like ['Hello', ',', 'world', '!']. Solution: Filter out punctuation using regular expressions or use nltk.sent_tokenize() to split sentences before tokenizing. 2. Over-stemming: Mistake: Using aggressive stemming that can reduce words like "better" to "bet" or "running" to "run" incorrectly. Solution: Use stemming selectively; consider lemmatization for more accurate results. 3. Handling special characters: Mistake: Failing to remove or correctly handle special characters (e.g. #, @, &), which may introduce noise in analysis. Solution: Use regular expressions or nltk.re methods to remove or properly handle special characters and symbols. 4. Ignoring Case Sensitivity: Mistake: Treating "Python" and "python" as different tokens. Solution: Convert text to lowercase (text.lower()) before tokenizing to avoid case discrepancies.

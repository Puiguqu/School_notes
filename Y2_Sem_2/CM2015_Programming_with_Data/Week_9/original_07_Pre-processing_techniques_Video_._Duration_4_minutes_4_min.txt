In this video, we will look at more techniques to clean data and use it for analysis. The first technique that we are looking at is called stemming. Stemming is the process of taking words and in their inflected forms and reducing it down to a stem word. For instance, words like programmers, programming and programs can be reduced to the stem word which is program. NLTK has two main stemmers that are available. One is called the Porter Stemmer and the other is called the Lancaster Stemmer. While the Porter Stemmer tends to focus on accuracy, the Lancaster Stemmer is much quicker and is a little more aggressive in its stemming. Let's use both these stemmers on the cleaned version of the tokens and compare the results. One observation is that the Porter Stemmer maintains the frequency distribution as close as possible to the original distribution. Additionally, it has added two new words to this list which are dance, D-A-N-C which is a stemmed form of probably dance or dancing and move which is a stemmed form of moves or move. On the other hand, we find that the distribution generated from the Lancaster Stemmer seems to be a little different from the frequency distribution of the original words. And the reason for this is that Lancaster Stemmer might have stemmed words a little more than is needed in this case. For instance, we see that the word American is no longer the most frequently occurring word. The word Martial has also been reduced to the word Mart which probably means a different thing. So based on the two outputs that we have, I would say that the Porter Stemmer in this particular instance seem to be doing better than the Lancaster Stemmer. A different technique from stemming which is also used to reduce words to their essential forms is called lemmatizer. Lemmatizer takes different words and reduces it to their root form. For this particular task, we are importing the WordNet lemmatizer. The WordNet is one of the largest corpuses of text that is available in the NLTK library. We are going to use the WordNet lemmatizer on the tokens that we have and use the lemmatizer to find their root forms. Let us review the results of the lemmatizer. We find that the output of the frequency distribution output of the lemmatizer is also quite similar to the original frequency distribution. However, while it has retained several categories such as American famously and Martial from the original, it has also added a new category called move which probably is a result of finding the root forms of different versions of move such as moves, move into one root word. However, the WordNet lemmatizer introduces only one category compared to the Porter Stemmer which has introduced two new categories in the frequency distribution. As a result, for this particular task, perhaps the Porter Stemmer might be more suited. In general, a good rule of thumb for preprocessing is to try out different stemming and lemmatizing techniques, compare them and find out which is more suited for your task.

This module requires students to engage in independent research activities. These activities mandate personal investigation using both the general internet and the dedicated Online Library. Crucially, participants must complete the Online Library tutorial before commencing the first peer review activity scheduled for the following week. Students are encouraged to utilize the Online Library's specialized pages for effective searching of databases and advanced information skills. A key technical instruction concerns accessing the library: one must log in using only the portion of the Portal username that precedes the "@" symbol. For example, if the full username is ab123@london.ac.uk, the user must enter ab123. Furthermore, reading the independent research checklist (independent research checklist.pdf) is strongly recommended to identify suitable internet resources. The focus area for this investigation should particularly involve gathering useful resources related to computer security. Students are also advised to enhance their academic writing and critical thinking skills through the Core Study Skills course. These skills will prove highly beneficial when eventually writing a report for peer review submission.

A second core component involves initiating a comprehensive study journal. This journal serves as a personal record for collating information, monitoring progress, and organizing study notes throughout the module. This journal is designated for reference purposes only and will not be included in the assessed grading. Regarding format, while a traditional pen and paper method is acceptable, electronic online documents are highly preferable. This preference is due to the superior accessibility of online documents from various computers, which is useful if studying from multiple locations. Several acceptable online document types include Word doc, Google doc, OneNote, a personal blog, and Evernote. Study journal entries must incorporate diverse materials such as collated links, articles, and references. They should also document personal thoughts and reflections on the material encountered. Students must complete directed activities, including case studies, alongside making notes on readings and videos. Structurally, the journal can be organized chronologically, labeling each section by week, perhaps numbering through 1–20. Within each weekly section, pages should be dedicated to activities, reflections, mini lecture notes, readings, and links. Functionality should include the ability to attach external document files for later reference. Additionally, incorporating a to-do list feature is advised for effective progress tracking and task completion.

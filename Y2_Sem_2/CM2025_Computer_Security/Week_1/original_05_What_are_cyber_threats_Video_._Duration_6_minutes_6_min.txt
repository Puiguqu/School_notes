My name is Dr. Roja Ahmadi and I want to welcome you to this session on one of the most critical topics in our digital age, cyber threats. We live in a world where so much of our personal and professional lives happen online, from banking to shopping, from education to healthcare. But with these advancements come risks. Cyber threats are no longer just a concept for big corporations or tech experts. They affect each and every one of us. Whether you're browsing social media, checking emails, or using online services, you are a potential target. Today, we're going to break down what cyber threats are, how they impact us, and what simple steps we can take to stay protected. What are cyber threats? In simple terms, a cyber threat is any attempt to gain unauthorized access to a system, network, or digital device with the intent to cause harm. These threats can come in many different forms and can target individuals, businesses, and even governments. Let's go through some of the most common types such as malware, phishing, ransomware, social engineering, information manipulation, and interference, supply chain attacks. Malware. Malware is short for malicious software. Think of it like a virus for your computer. It can steal data, spy on your activity, or even destroy files. Phishing. Ever receive an email that looks legitimate, but it's actually trying to trick you into giving you a password or credit card information. That's called phishing. It's one of the most common and successful cyber threats. Ransomware. Imagine your files are locked and a message appears asking you to pay money to get them back. That's ransomware. It's a growing and very costly threat. Social engineering. Social engineering isn't a technical attack, but a psychological one. It's when someone manipulates you into revealing confidential information. It could be as simple as someone pretending to be tech support to gain access to your computer. Information manipulation and interference. Information manipulation and interference includes spreading false information or electing legitimate data to cause confusion, influence decisions, or disrupt systems. These types of threats can impact elections, financial markets, and public trust. Supply chain attacks. Supply chain attacks target less secure elements in the supply chain. Hackers might infiltrate third-party vendors to eventually access a large organization system. SolarWinds attack is a prime example where attackers compromise software updates to infiltrate major institutions. In this attack, the hackers inject malware into the legitimate software update, which was then pushed to thousands of customers, including major government agencies. The attacker remains undetected for months. To understand the real-world impact of these threats, let's look at a couple of examples. In 2017, the WannaCry ransomware attack infected over 200,000 computers in more than 150 countries. It hit hospitals in the UK, forcing surgeries to be canceled. The attackers demand payment in Bitcoin to unlock the files. It caused billions of dollars in damage. In the Equifix data breach in the same year, hackers stole personal data, including a social security number of nearly 150 million people. That's almost half of the U.S. population. The breach happened because of the vulnerability in update software. These examples aren't isolated incidents. Every day, thousands of people and businesses face attacks, even small businesses. And every day, users are at risk. These examples show us why it's so important to understand and defend against cyber threats. Why is cyber security important? Cyber security is crucial because cyber threats affect everyone. On a personal level, it could mean losing control of your personal data. For businesses, the stakes are even higher.

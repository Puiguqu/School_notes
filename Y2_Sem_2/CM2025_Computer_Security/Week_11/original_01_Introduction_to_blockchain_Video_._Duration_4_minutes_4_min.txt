Today we'll take a beginner's look at Blockchain to give you a solid foundation to understand how Blockchain supports cybersecurity and digital trust. By the end of this session, you should be able to explain Blockchain, describe how it functions and identify its essential components such as blocks, hashes and nodes. These are fundamental concepts that will help you in understanding future applications. Blockchain is a shared digital ledger, that means it's like a database, but it's stored across many computers rather than just one. Each piece of data is stored in a block and these blocks are linked together in a chain. Once data is added, it cannot be easily changed, making it very secure. Some of the most important features of Blockchain are that it's distributed, secure and transparent. Every computer in the network has a copy of the same information. New data is added in the blocks and it's difficult to alter. Once verified, these blocks are linked to previous ones, creating a permanent change of records that everyone can view. Let's look at how Blockchain works. First, someone requests a transaction. This request is broadcast to a network of computers. These computers, called nodes, validate the request. Once validated, the transaction is grouped with others to form a block. This block is then added to the Blockchain and the update is shared with the entire network. Every Blockchain is made up of key parts. Each block contains data, a timestamp and cryptographic hash. The hash is like a fingerprint for that block. Blocks are linked together using the hash of the previous block. Nodes are the computers that maintain the Blockchain. The ledger is the full records of all confirmed transactions. Blockchain matters because it improves security, builds trust and removes the need for a central authority. It is being used in finance, healthcare, supply chain and many other areas where trust and record integrity are important. By using Blockchain, we can create systems that are transparent and difficult to corrupt. Blockchain is powerful, but it's not perfect. Some systems use a lot of energy. Handling large numbers of transactions quickly can be a challenge. Also it is still developing in terms of laws and standards. These are important points to consider before using Blockchain in a real-world system. To summarize, Blockchain is a secure, shared system for recording transactions. It works by linking data blocks together in a chain across a network of computers. The core parts include blocks, hashes, nodes and ledgers.

We look at how these self-executing agreements are changing the way we do business and exchange value. After this session, you'll understand what smart contracts are, how they work on blockchain, their automation and feature trust, where they're already making an impact in industries like finance, supply chain, and healthcare, their risks and limitations. A smart contract is a self-executing program with the terms coded into software. It's sold on a blockchain that automatically executes when its predefined conditions are met. It's transparent, tamper-proof, and runs without human intervention. Once deployed, smart contracts cannot be changed. They are coded using languages like Solidity and triggered by inputs or transactions on the blockchain, for example, Ethereum. This makes them ideal for enforcing rules and automating actions. These contracts reduce operational costs by removing the need for intermediaries or manual validation, automate processes, ensure improved speed and accuracy, and build trust by making the process transparent and tamper-proof. Industries are already benefiting. In finance, smart contracts handle automated payments and insurance claims. In healthcare, they help manage consent and medical data sharing. Supply chains use them to track goods and verify product origins. Real estate deals are made entirely digital. Legally, they are used for school services and digital contracts. However, the following risks and limitations exist. If there is a bug in the code, the contract may behave unpredictably. The code is final, errors cannot be reversed, the scalability and networks are limited. Legal enforcement varies and regulations are still evolving. Consider the flight delay insurance example. A smart contract checks the status of flight via an API. If a flight is late, the contract fetches data via the API and pays compensation automatically. There is no claim process and no human error. In conclusion, smart contracts represent a leap forward in secure automated transactions. Their continued development will redefine digital trust and business operations. And their growing adoption signals a shift in how agreements are managed.

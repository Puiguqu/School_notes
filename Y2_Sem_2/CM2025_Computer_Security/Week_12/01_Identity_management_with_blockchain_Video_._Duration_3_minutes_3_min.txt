**Blockchain for Identity Management and Cybersecurity Curriculum**

The module addresses Identity Management with Blockchain, aiming to solve the inherent inefficiencies, risks, and privacy concerns of traditional identity systems. Current traditional systems rely on vulnerable, centralized databases susceptible to breaches and identity theft. In these systems, users often possess limited control over their personal data, and verification processes are typically slow and inefficient. Blockchain introduces decentralized identity systems, fundamentally enabling self-sovereign identity (SSI). SSI empowers users with full control over their credentials, facilitating secure and transparent authentication without relying on a central authority. Identity data is created and verified, then stored on the blockchain as encrypted hashes. Users retain the crucial ability to grant or revoke access to their credentials at any point. This mechanism makes verification processes instantly secure, fully traceable, and efficient. The core benefits of blockchain identity include enhanced security, improved privacy, reduced risk of fraud, and easier cross-border verification. Practical use cases span governmental digital identity services, financial inclusion, medical record authentication, secure online voting, and verifiable academic credential management. However, the adoption of this technology faces significant challenges, including the need for industry standardization and navigating complex regulatory compliance. Another technical hurdle involves the secure management and safeguarding of private cryptographic keys.

These help us understand how adversaries operate and how we can defend against them After this session you'll be able to describe the stages of cyber kill chain explain the structure of MITRE framework compare their structures and purposes use them to enhance detection and response strategies The cyber kill chain created by Lockheed Martin outlines seven steps that adversaries follow during a cyber attack The goal is to detect and disrupt the attack as early as possible The stages of cyber kill chain are reconnaissance, weaponization, delivery, exploitation, installation, command and control, and action on objectives Recognizing these helps defenders break the chain early Security teams map defenses to each phase of the change It's useful for building layered defenses helping teams understand adversary behavior mapping defensive tools to specific stages For example, email filters, address delivery, and point protection tackle installation and network monitoring detects command and control channels MITRE framework categorizes attacker action into tactics, high-level attackers' goals, for example, initial access, execution techniques, how goals are achieved, for example, phishing, scripting Subtechniques provide even more granularity and MITRE framework updates this database regularly Security operation teams use MITRE to assess where they have visibility gaps and to improve detection rules It helps red and blue teams simulate and defend against attacks It's also used for threat hunting and SOC maturity and training While the kill chain offers a linear narrative MITRE is a dynamic matrix The kill chain helps visualize overall attack flow MITRE offers details Together they support proactive and reactive defense Let's consider a phishing attack The kill chain shows us that email delivery and execution were involved MITRE helps us pinpoint specific techniques such as initial access via spear phishing, execution with malicious scripts and the analysis of the persistence mechanism using the registry key Combining both models enhances incidence response analysis In summary, both models provide a structured ways to understand cyber threats The cyber kill chain simplifies attack flow while MITRE framework enriches detection with practical data In our next session, we'll dive into digital forensic analysis ## END TRANSCRIPT ## ##

Some screen readers may require using CTRL in conjunction with the alt key Welcome to the session on AI Power Threat Detection. As cyber threats grow in volume and sophistication, artificial intelligence has become an essential tool in the security arsenal. After this session, you'll be able to explain how AI is used in cybersecurity to support threat detection, identify key AI techniques in cybersecurity, explore use cases and benefits recognized challenges and limitations, understand integration with security operations. The volume and speed of modern threats are overwhelming for human analysts. AI helps by enabling faster detection, response and automation, and improving accuracy and reducing false positives. AI techniques include machine learning, which can recognize patterns and anomalies. Deep learning, which helps to analyze neural networks for complex behaviors. NLP, which helps to interpret textual data like phishing images or logs. And behavior analytics, which detects deviations in user or system activity. AI is widely applied in intrusion detection systems, IDS, with ML algorithms. Pitching detection through NLP-based email scanners. Malware classification using deep learning. And insider threat detection via behavioral profiling. These tools reduce manual work while catching threats that would otherwise be missed. With AI, threat detection is faster, given real-time analysis and alerting. It can scale across vast networks. It enables proactive defense, especially against zero-day threats or insider activity. It also reduces analyst workloads and makes the Security Operations Center, SoC, more efficient. However, AI is not perfect. It requires high-quality training data. Models need clean data and can be tricked by adversaries. Over-reliance may lead to misused outliers. And not all tools are easily explainable. Human oversight remains important. AI tools are integrated into modern systems, XDR platforms, using ML for cross-layer detection, and SOAR platforms. They can automate early triage steps and assist analysts by highlighting true positives faster and more accurately. Consider an example. An organization deploys an AI-enhanced email gateway scanner that scans all incoming emails using NLP. NLP flags a phishing message based on tone, length, and timing. The system alerts the SoC and blocks the message in real-time. In conclusion, AI enhances threat detection by enabling speed, precision, and insight. However, it must be paired with robust strategy and human validation.

Some screen readers may require using CTRL in conjunction with the alt key Welcome to this session on why EDI matters. In this lesson, we'll explore how equality, diversity and inclusion, known as an EDI, are crucial to building fair, secure and effective digital systems. By the end of this session, you'll be able to define EDI and its relevance in cybersecurity. Identify how a lack of diversity can create security and fairness issues. Recognize the value of inclusive, design and diverse perspective. EDI stands for Equality, Diversity and Inclusion. equality means giving everyone fair access to opportunities. Diversity is about representing different people, cultures, genders and backgrounds. Inclusion means making sure that diverse voices are heard and valued. In cybersecurity, teams that lack diversity may unintentionally overlook vulnerabilities or ignore user groups. This can result in systems that don't work properly or securely for everyone. Diversity helps to close these gaps. Here are some examples for real-world biases. Facial recognition systems often fail or darker skin tone due to non-diverse training data. Hiring algorithms have been found to favor men over women. Predictive policy models unfairly target certain communities. These aren't just technical errors, they are ethical issues. Having a diverse team means more ideas, better risk identification and fewer blind spots. It fosters creativity and encourages questioning assumptions, which leads to more robust digital systems. Bias in AI and machine learning systems often stems from biased data or a lack of diverse input during system design. If the people building the technology don't represent the people using it, the results can be discriminatory or unsafe. Failing to consider EDI can also have legal consequences. GDPR, the Equality Act and other AI ethics frameworks now require fairness and accountability. Inclusive design is no longer optional, it's expected. Consider the following questions. Have you encountered biased technology? What was the impact? What steps can teams take to ensure inclusiveness? What prevents company technology teams from becoming more diverse? To wrap up, EDI matters because it helps us build digital systems that are fair, secure and usable for everyone. Diverse teams create better solutions. Inclusive design prevents harm.

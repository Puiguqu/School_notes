# Activity – Career development plan
Reading•
. Duration: 1 hour
1h - Summary

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-cm2025-computer-security/supplement/LBFmI/activity-career-development-plan

Cybersecurity is characterized as a fast-moving field requiring a blend of technical and professional proficiencies. Technically, expertise must cover systems and network security, encryption, and secure coding practices. Equally critical are professional skills and mindset attributes, including ethics, inclusivity, sustainability, and effective communication. To plan a career path, one must systematically map out goals, required skills, and achievable milestones. The ultimate task is developing a comprehensive career development plan, presented as a timeline, flowchart, or written format (400–500 words). This plan must integrate both core technical skills and professional competencies, such as global employability skills. The planning process begins by defining the "Starting Point" and identifying the current career stage. Individuals must self-assess if they are a career starter, a career changer, or a career developer. Furthermore, one must outline existing strengths, covering both technical knowledge and transferable skills, such as complex problem solving and decision-making.

Next, the plan requires defining "Short-term goals," spanning one to two years. These goals are tailored based on the starting stage. Starters should target entry-level roles, internships, or foundational certifications. Career changers must identify bridging roles or upskilling opportunities built upon their existing expertise. Developers, conversely, should aim for advanced certifications or leadership advancement. During this phase, specific module skills like networking, encryption, secure coding, and EDI awareness must be actively strengthened.

The "Medium-term goals" (3–5 years) outline intermediate professional growth. Examples include becoming a penetration tester, SOC analyst, or cloud security specialist. Success at this stage requires planned professional development, potentially involving certifications, postgraduate studies, or industry memberships. For "Long-term goals" (5+ years), one must visualize achieving senior roles such as Security Architect, CISO, or Policy Advisor. Maintaining employability demands continuous learning and adherence to ethical, inclusive leadership practices.

The final critical step involves identifying 3–4 concrete "Milestones and resources" for the duration of the degree. These milestones are specific actions, like completing foundational certifications such as CompTIA Security+ or CCNA. Practical milestones include building a small portfolio project, such as coding a secure application, or participating in a lab-based penetration test. Suggested resources include the University of London Careers Service and virtual experience platforms like Forage. Technical training can also be achieved through GitHub hackathons or Capture the Flag competitions. These resources collectively support the development of global employability skills, such as presenting complex technical findings to non-technical audiences.

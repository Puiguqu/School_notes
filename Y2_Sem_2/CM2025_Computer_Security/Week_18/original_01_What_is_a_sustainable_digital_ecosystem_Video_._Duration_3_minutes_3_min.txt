Some screen readers may require using CTRL in conjunction with the alt key Welcome to this session on what is a sustainable digital ecosystem. We'll explore how technology, people, and the planet must align to create systems that last. By the end of this session, you'll be able to define a sustainable digital ecosystem, recognize its key components and relationships, explore principles of resilience, adaptability, and ethics, understand its impact on cybersecurity, the economy, and the environment. A digital ecosystem is a network of interconnected technologies, services, platforms, and users that interact digitally. Think of cloud networks, smart cities, and connected health systems functioning like a natural ecosystem that is dynamic, interdependent, and evolving. A sustainable digital ecosystem has long-term viability through its energy efficiency, security, economic accessibility, and minimal environmental impact. For digital ecosystems to be considered sustainable, they need to have 1. Technologically robust, secure, and stable systems 2. Environmentally conscious energy use in data centers and a green design 3. Economically fair access and digital equality 4. Social and ethical user trust, data privacy, and inclusion If these systems are poorly designed, they may have a high carbon footprint by consuming too much energy, exclude vulnerable populations, or become vulnerable to attack. That's why sustainability is crucial, not optional. A strong cybersecurity maintains ecosystem integrity. It prevents exploitation of critical infrastructure and protects users, data, and trust in digital systems, which are key to sustainability. To build a sustainable digital ecosystem, we must 1. Use green energy and sustainable IT 2. Design open systems for interoperability 3. Have resilient architectures with zero-trust models 4. Use AI ethically 5. Have responsible data governance In conclusion, a sustainable digital ecosystem 1. Balanced technology growth with environmental, economic, and ethical responsibilities 2. Supports secure, fair, and responsible innovation The next session will be the anatomy of real-world cybersecurity incidents. ## END TRANSCRIPT ## ## ADDITIONAL PAGE CONTENT ## ### Week 1 Week 1 - Topic 1: Identification and classification of cyber threats (part 1) ### Week 2 Week 2 -

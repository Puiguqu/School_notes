# Activity – Career development plan
Reading•
. Duration: 1 hour
1h

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-cm2025-computer-security/supplement/LBFmI/activity-career-development-plan

Cybersecurity is a fast-moving field that demands both strong technical expertise (e.g., systems and network security, encryption, secure coding) and mindset and skills (ethics, inclusivity, sustainability and communication). To plan your future career, it is useful to map out your goals, the skills you need and the milestones that will help you achieve them. You might find it useful to refer to the previous reading and the research you've already done around certifications in your area to help develop your plan. Task Create a career development plan that sets out your pathway into and through the cybersecurity profession, making sure that you focus on both technical skills and professional skills (e.g. the global employability skills). Present your plan as a timeline, flowchart, roadmap diagram or written plan (400–500 words). 1. Starting point Identify your current career stage: are you a career starter (new to the field), a career changer (moving from another profession into cyber) or a career developer (already working in the field and aiming to progress)? Outline the skills, experiences and strengths you already have at this stage – both technical and transferable (e.g. global employability skills, such as complex problem solving, decision-making and communication). 2. Short-term goals (1–2 years) Define what ‘next steps’ look like for your career stage. Starters: consider entry-level roles, internships or foundational certifications. Changers: identify bridging roles, upskilling opportunities or specialist training that builds on your existing expertise. Developers: target advancement through new responsibilities, leadership roles or advanced certifications. Identify which skills from this module (e.g., networking, encryption, secure coding, EDI awareness) you want to strengthen. 3. Medium-term goals (3–5 years) Where do you see yourself after some growth in cyber (e.g., penetration tester, SOC analyst, digital forensics, cloud security specialist, team lead)? What professional development (certifications, postgraduate study, industry memberships) will support this stage? 4. Long-term goals (5+ years) What senior or leadership roles could you aim for (e.g., security architect, CISO, policy advisor)? How will you maintain sustainability and employability – continuous learning, ethical practice, inclusive leadership? 5. Milestones and resources Identify 3–4 short-term milestones you can work towards during your degree. These should focus on actions that will strengthen your readiness for a career in cybersecurity. For example: completing an entry-level or bridging certification (e.g., CompTIA Security+, CCNA or cloud fundamentals) building a small portfolio project (e.g., coding a secure application, running a penetration test in a lab or contributing to an open-source tool) attending a University of London Careers Service expert panel on cyber security, or other local cybersecurity industry events joining a professional body (where relevant) developing your global employability skills, such as presenting technical findings to non-technical audiences. List the resources you will use to achieve these milestones, such as online training platforms, university careers services, mentorship opportunities or professional networks. Here are some ideas to get you started: your University of London Careers Service resources virtual internships and taster experiences, such as those available at Forage technology communities and training platforms such as GitHub hackathons and Capture the Flag competitions.

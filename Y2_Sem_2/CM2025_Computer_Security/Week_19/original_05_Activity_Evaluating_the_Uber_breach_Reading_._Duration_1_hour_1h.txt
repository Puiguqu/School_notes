# Activity – Evaluating the Uber breach
Reading•
. Duration: 1 hour
1h

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-cm2025-computer-security/supplement/zFaoB/activity-evaluating-the-uber-breach

The Uber breach of 2022 exposed how poor credential management, hard-coded admin passwords and weak internal access controls can undermine enterprise security. In this assignment you will critically evaluate the breach using principles of secure system design and propose improvements that could prevent similar incidents in the future. Task Write a structured response (800–1,000 words) in your study journal that addresses the following points. Design flaws Identify the key design flaws that contributed to the Uber breach. Explain how principles of secure system design (e.g., least privilege, zero trust, network segmentation) could have prevented or reduced the risk. Redesign of access management Propose how you would redesign Uber’s internal access management to improve resilience. Include an outline of the policies, tools or frameworks that you would implement (e.g., identity and access management systems, multi-factor authentication, role-based access controls). Testing and monitoring Recommend what security testing or monitoring processes (e.g., penetration testing, red teaming, continuous monitoring, anomaly detection) could have detected early signs of the attack. Analyse why Uber’s monitoring failed and what changes could strengthen detection.

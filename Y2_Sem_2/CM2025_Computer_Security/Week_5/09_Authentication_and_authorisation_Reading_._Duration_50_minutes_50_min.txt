Building on your understanding of access control models and the role of intrusion detection and prevention systems (IDS/IPS), this paper explores multi-factor authentication (MFA) as a complementary mechanism for strengthening system security. While access control defines who can enter and IDS/IPS monitor for suspicious activity once systems are in use, MFA reinforces the initial gateway by demanding more than just a password for login.

# OS security
Reading•
. Duration: 1 hour 55 minutes
1h 55m

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-cm2025-computer-security/supplement/DQA0D/os-security

The purpose of this reading is to examine why mainstream operating systems such as UNIX and Windows fall short of secure design requirements. It highlights the historical development of their protection mechanisms, explores their inherent weaknesses, and illustrates how common vulnerabilities create persistent risks in digital infrastructures. Read: Jaeger, T. Operating system security. (Cham: Springer Nature Switzerland, 2022) Chapter 4: Security in ordinary operating systems. This reading is in the Online Library in the VLeBooks Collection. Find out how to search for the book . As you read, focus on: tracing how protection systems in UNIX and Windows evolved noting the criteria that ordinary OSs fail to meet reflecting on how vulnerabilities illustrate the need for more secure system design If you would like to read more in depth about operating system security, you could read chapters 5–8 of this reading.

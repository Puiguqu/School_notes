# Topic 3 summary

This topic has focused on the critical relationship between operating systems and networks in maintaining secure digital environments and how they work together to support cybersecurity from the ground up. So far, you have explored: network and OS layers. You have learned how network and operating system layers interact, and why this interaction is critical for building secure digital infrastructures intrusion detection, prevention and access control. You have studied IDS and IPS, compared their strengths and limitations, and explored access control models (DAC, MAC, RBAC, ABAC) as key mechanisms for protecting systems and data practical monitoring and OS hardening. You have practised using Wireshark to analyse live network traffic and engaged with hands-on exercises in Windows and Linux security, including hardening, auditing and responding to breaches caused by poor configuration. By completing practical scenarios and assignments, you are understanding how system misconfigurations and weak security practices can lead to serious breaches. By studying Windows and Linux operating systems, you are learning about their importance in security management and defence.

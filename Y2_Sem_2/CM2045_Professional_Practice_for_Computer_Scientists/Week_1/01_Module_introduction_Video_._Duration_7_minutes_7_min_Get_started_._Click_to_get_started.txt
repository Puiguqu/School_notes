This module, Professional Practice for Computer Scientists, aims to equip students with essential professional skills crucial for success in the rapidly evolving tech industry. Led by Dr. Sean McGrath, the course focuses on three core pillars: effective communication, ethical responsibility, and continuous professional development. While technical expertise is fundamental, the industry requires broader skills, such as project management and understanding ethical/legal implications. The module teaches students to communicate both technical and non-technical concepts to diverse audiences. Key communication areas covered include written documentation, verbal presentations, and impactful visual aids. Students will learn to write technical reports, such as creating an executive summary for a machine learning paper, and tailoring communication for specific contexts. A major focus is placed on the ethical, legal, and sustainability issues affecting computing. Topics include data privacy, intellectual property, and the environmental impact of technology, requiring students to approach work responsibly. Effective teamwork strategies are vital, emphasizing collaboration within diverse teams with varying skill sets. The curriculum also introduces project management techniques, detailing concepts and theories behind industry-standard methodologies like Agile. Students will learn best practices in task prioritization, communication, and conflict resolution to ensure successful project outcomes. Furthermore, the module guides students through career planning, emphasizing lifelong learning and setting goals for continuous professional improvement. The course is structured across multiple weeks (e.g., Week 1, Week 5, Week 9, Week 11) and is set at Level 5, demanding a higher level of academic reflection. The content progresses through distinct topics, including Technical Writing (Week 2), Presentation Skills (Week 3 & 4), Legal/Ethical Foundations (Week 5 & 6), Inclusive Computing (Week 7 & 8), and Sustainability (Week 9 & 10). Specific, technical skills developed include mastery of advanced technical documentation and report creation. Assessment components emphasize developing written and communication skills, including opportunities for reflection on growth. Resources available span video lectures, readings, discussion forums, and career resources, supporting active peer and instructor engagement. Students are advised to utilize the Career Service and industry partners for practical advice. Successful completion ensures students can articulate complex technical concepts to both technical and non-technical audiences. Graduates will be prepared to manage projects using industry-standard methodologies and understand computing's legal and ethical boundaries. The overall goal is not merely passing a course, but building a strong foundation for a professional career. Ultimately, the module positions the student as a future leader, innovator, and engineer in the field of computing.

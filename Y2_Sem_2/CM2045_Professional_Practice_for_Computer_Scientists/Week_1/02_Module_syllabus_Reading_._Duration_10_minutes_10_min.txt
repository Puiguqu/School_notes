This module, CM204, Professional Practice for Computer Scientists, serves as a critical bridge between theoretical computer science knowledge and essential practical skills. It aims to develop critical judgment in students through the lens of law, ethics, and environmental sustainability. This approach fosters a holistic understanding of computing's profound societal impacts. A major emphasis is placed on effective communication, collaborative teamwork, and proficient project management techniques. These skills collectively prepare students for real-world problem-solving and system development within diverse teams. The course emphasizes refining professional practice, preparing students for post-graduation computing careers. It introduces foundational ethical, legal, and regulatory principles, promoting responsible computing practices across all domains. Students will significantly refine their communication skills, which are vital for successful teamwork and stakeholder engagement. Furthermore, the module explores the critical themes of sustainability, ethics, and comprehensive risk management in modern computing. Specifically, students are taught methods to accurately identify and proactively mitigate potential technical and operational issues. The curriculum also covers crucial industry standards and best practices necessary for ensuring consistent and high-quality professional work. Teamwork strategies are taught, focusing on effective methods for operating within large, collaborative project structures. The module structure culminates with dedicated components on career planning and embracing a trajectory of lifelong learning. It integrates embedded career development insights and expert content from the University of London Careers Service. This supplemental material highlights the value of developing Global Employability Skills. Students receive comprehensive guidance on formulating their personal career strategies, regardless of their current stage or geographical region. Upon successful completion, students gain the ability to communicate both technical and non-technical concepts clearly and effectively. They can utilize written, verbal, and visual mediums while adhering to proper professional etiquette. Students will be able to explain key laws, regulations, and ethical codes applicable to the profession. Furthermore, they must apply these laws and codes to example scenarios to demonstrate compliant practices. A key learning outcome is the ability to recognize and discuss sustainability impacts, such as e-waste and energy usage. Students are required to propose improvements that are rigorously aligned with ethical principles. Graduates must also evaluate computing systems and data for inherent biases and accessibility issues. This involves recommending inclusive solutions that robustly support equality, diversity, and inclusion. Finally, students will master the application of professional risk management processes. This skill involves the accurate identification, thorough analysis, and effective mitigation of risks and uncertainties within a computing project scenario.

# Transdisciplinary approach to sustainability
Reading•
. Duration: 30 minutes
30 min

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-cm2045-professional-practice-for-computer-scientists/supplement/CFjej/transdisciplinary-approach-to-sustainability

Technology presents both challenges and opportunities for sustainability. On the one hand, rapid technological advancements can lead to increased resource consumption, electronic waste and environmental degradation. For instance, the production and disposal of electronic devices contributes significantly to pollution and resource depletion. On the other hand, technology offers innovative solutions to sustainability issues. Renewable energy technologies, such as solar and wind power, reduce reliance on fossil fuels and lower carbon emissions. Advancements in smart grids, energy-efficient systems and sustainable agriculture practices also enhance resource management and reduce the environmental impact. Balancing these challenges and opportunities is crucial for achieving a sustainable future. Artificial Intelligence (AI) poses several environmental challenges. One major issue is the high energy consumption required to train and run AI models. Data centres, which house AI servers, consume vast amounts of electricity, contributing to significant carbon emissions. In addition, the production and disposal of electronic components used in AI infrastructure generates electronic waste and requires critical minerals, often mined unsustainably. Another challenge is the water usage in cooling systems for data centres, which can strain local water resources. Balancing the benefits of AI with these environmental impacts is crucial for sustainable development. Environmental dilemmas can be very complex and require a range of perspectives to address effectively. Bringing together professionals who can share viewpoints from different technical and non-technical disciplines to solve complex problems is often described as transdisciplinary working. Transdisciplinary human teams are a great way to leverage new technologies to deliver the organisational strategy. They deliver enhanced and creative solutions to tricky dilemmas and are less prone to group think and blind spots in problem articulation and solution. They are also more likely to surface the impact on a fuller range of stakeholders and users of any solution proposed. Employers in the tech space increasingly look for transdisciplinary working skills in their new recruits and their aspiring leaders. You need to show that you can listen to others, identify the professional or cultural lenses or biases that you and others may bring to the situation, and that you can work effectively with colleagues who bring very different perspectives to dilemmas that you may face at work. You can practise your transdisciplinary skills at our regular University of London Careers Service webinars which focus on collaborative problem solving and transdisciplinary thinking. A sample webinar is available: Global Dilemmas: Environmental Impact As you listen to the webinar, take a transdisciplinary approach and consider the different lenses through which you may be viewing this content Your academic lens: which technology specialism interests you most? Your lived experience lens: where in the world are you now and what is your previous experience? Your career stage lens: how much work experience do you currently have; are you a career starter, career developer or a career changer? How might these different perspectives affect how you look at sustainability dilemmas in your professional life?

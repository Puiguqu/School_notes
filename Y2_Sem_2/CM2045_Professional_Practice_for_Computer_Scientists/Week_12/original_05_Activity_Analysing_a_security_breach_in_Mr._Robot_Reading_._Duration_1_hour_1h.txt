# Activity – Analysing a security breach in Mr. Robot
Reading•
. Duration: 1 hour
1h

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-cm2045-professional-practice-for-computer-scientists/supplement/o1NwU/activity-analysing-a-security-breach-in-mr-robot

Mr. Robot is an American TV show about a cybersecurity engineer and hacker who is recruited to join a group of hacktivists called ‘fsociety’. The group aims to take down corrupt corporations through cyber attacks. In this activity, you’ll analyse a security breach depicted in the show and identify the systemic failures that contributed to the breach and propose mitigation strategies. The goals of this activity are: To apply your understanding of cybersecurity principles to a fictional scenario. To analyse the causes and consequences of security breaches. To develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills in the context of cybersecurity. To gain a deeper appreciation for the complexities of cybersecurity in a technology-driven world. Instructions Choose a clip from Mr. Robot which depicts a security breach. We recommend the clips below, but you can choose another one if you can’t access these. You can either find these on YouTube or read episode descriptions online (e.g. at Epguides, IMDB). Season 1, Episode 5: fsociety hacks Steel Mountain. This clip shows the fsociety group infiltrating a secure data facility. Season 1, Episode 1: Elliot hacks Evil Corp. This clip depicts Elliot's initial hack of E Corp, exposing vulnerabilities and setting the stage for further attacks. There are lots more sophisticated hacks in the later episodes. Please note: if you struggle to find the clips of Mr. Robot online, you can choose another fictional scenario (from a TV show, book or film) that depicts a cyber security breach and still complete this activity. Carefully watch the selected clip or read the description and analyse the events leading to the security breach. Pay attention to the following: Technical vulnerabilities: What weaknesses in the systems or technology were exploited? (e.g., weak passwords, unpatched software, insecure network configurations) Human factors: What role did human error or social engineering play in the breach? (e.g., phishing, negligence, insider threats) Process failures: Were there any shortcomings in security policies, procedures, or incident response plans? (e.g., lack of access controls, inadequate monitoring, poor security awareness training) Write a short report (approximately 500 words) addressing the following: Summary of the breach: Briefly describe the security breach depicted in the clip. Systemic failures: Identify the key technical, human and process failures that contributed to the breach. Provide specific examples from the clip to support your analysis. Mitigation strategies: Propose realistic and effective mitigation strategies to address the identified failures. Consider both technical solutions (e.g., stronger authentication, encryption, intrusion detection systems) and non-technical measures (e.g., employee training, security awareness campaigns, improved incident response plans). Reflection: After completing your report, reflect on the following: Real-world relevance: How do the security breaches depicted in Mr. Robot relate to real-world cybersecurity threats and vulnerabilities? Do you think you might fall for any/all of these? It’s easy to assume as tech people we are ‘invulnerable’ to such vectors of attack, but imagine a time when you are tired, upset or just generally not at your best. Might you react differently? Human element: How does the show portray the human element in cybersecurity, and why is it often the weakest link? A clue to this might be to think about password complexity length. We are reaching a point at which it is becoming increasingly difficult to remember passwords, but this poses potential vectors for breaching our security (like writing passwords down.) Ethical considerations: What ethical dilemmas are raised in the show regarding hacking, privacy, and security? This activity encourages you to engage with cybersecurity concepts in a dynamic and engaging way, bridging the gap between theory and practice. By analysing a fictional security breach, you can develop a more nuanced understanding of the challenges and solutions in the real world of cybersecurity. I also think this show is brilliant!

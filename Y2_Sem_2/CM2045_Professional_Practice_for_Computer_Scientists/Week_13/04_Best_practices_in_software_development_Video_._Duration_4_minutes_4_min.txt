# Best practices in software development

1. Software development is a complex "journey from idea to finished product," requiring more than just coding.
2. IEEE 12.2.07 is a key standard that maps out this comprehensive journey: the software lifecycle.
3. The need for a lifecycle standard stems from the complexity of large systems, such as air traffic control or massive games.
4. A standard provides a common language and structural framework, ensuring quality and predictability across diverse teams.
5. The standard covers the entire scope, from initial requirements gathering to post-release maintenance.
6. Critically, 12.2.07 defines necessary processes, not specific technical coding methods.
7. It acts as an adaptable framework that can be tailored to varying project types and sizes.
8. The standard identifies five key sequential processes: Acquisition, Supply, Development, Operation, and Maintenance.
9. **Acquisition** focuses on defining the software's needs, involving resource gathering and initial planning.
10. **Supply** covers the actual building phase, incorporating essential steps like design, coding, and testing.
11. **Development** is the core coding stage, also encompassing crucial activities such as documentation and reviews.
12. **Operation** involves deploying the software, providing user support, and ensuring smooth continuous functioning.
13. **Maintenance** addresses long-term needs, including bug fixing, feature addition, and adapting to changing requirements.
14. These macro-processes are broken down further into distinct **activities**, such as requirements analysis within Acquisition.
15. Each activity is further detailed into specific **tasks**, which define actionable work items.
16. Each task requires defined **inputs** (what is needed to start) and must generate specific **outputs** (what is produced).
17. 12.2.07 places strong emphasis on documentation, which helps track progress, ensure consistency, and facilitate knowledge transfer.
18. Document types mandated include requirements documents, design documents, test plans, and user manuals.
19. A key technical function of the standard is **traceability**, which links different documents and activities to show system coherence.
20. Benefits of adoption include improved communication, reduced risk through structured adherence, and significantly higher quality software.
21. Furthermore, structured processes promote increased efficiency by streamlining development and reducing wasted effort.
22. The standard's principles are widely used by organizations developing critical systems, often leading to certification.
23. While traditionally structured, 12.2.07 can also be adapted to work with modern methodologies, including agile processes.
24. The standard encourages continuous improvement and ongoing evaluation across the entire software lifecycle.
25. Overall, adherence to standards ensures that software development remains predictable, systematic, and successful regardless of project scale.

The IEEE is a massive global organisation with over 400,000 members in 160 different countries, myself included. You are also able to become a student or a graduate member too, giving you access to lots of resources like the IEEE library. But what exactly do they do and why should we care? It was founded in 1884 as the AIEE with the mission of advancing technology for the benefit of humanity. Activities include developing standards, publishing technical literature, organising conferences and providing educational resources. It has a significant impact as IEEE standards touch almost every aspect of our lives, from the electricity that powers our homes to the Wi-Fi that connects us to the internet. IEEE standards development is open and consensus-based. Anyone can participate in the IEEE standards development process. A rigorous process involving multiple stages of review and approval to ensure quality and fairness. It has a global impact. IEEE standards are adopted worldwide, promoting interoperability and innovation. The key areas of focus are electrical engineering, power systems, renewable energy and electric vehicles. Electronics such as microelectronics, semiconductors and nanotechnology. Computer engineering, computer architecture, software engineering and cyber security. They even offer standards for usability and accessibility, my areas of research. Telecommunications, wireless communications, networking and internet technologies. IEEE 802 networking standards include Ethernet 802.3, the foundation of wired networking. They define the physical and data link layers of wired networks, ensuring reliable and efficient data transmission. Wi-Fi 802.11, the ubiquitous standard for wireless networking. It enables devices to connect to the internet without wires, providing flexibility and mobility. And Bluetooth 802.15.1, a standard for short range wireless communication, used in devices like headphones, keyboards and smart home devices. Other notable IEEE standards include IEEE 754. This defines the format for floating point numbers in computers, ensuring consistency in numerical computations. There are equally standards for things like date time representations, or what we often refer to as timestamps. IEEE 1394 or Firewire, a high speed serial bus standard for connecting devices like cameras and external hard drives. IEEE 1076 or VHDL, a hardware description language used to design and simulate digital circuits. There are really two types of standards, though you might find they interchange based on developments in tech and usage patterns. De facto, standards that emerge through widespread use and market dominance, like the QWERTY keyboard. De jour, formally approved standards like IEEE 802.11 for Wi-Fi. IEEE's role, many IEEE standards start as de facto and become de jour through the formal standardization process. The value of IEEE standards is to ensure interoperability, allowing different devices and systems to work together seamlessly. Innovation – to provide a common platform for innovation and development. Safety – ensures that electrical and electronic products conform to safety requirements. Consumer protection – guarantees a certain level of quality and performance. Some examples of standards in action include. Smart grid – IEEE standards are critical for the development of smart grids which enable more efficient and reliable energy distribution. Autonomous vehicles – IEEE standards are addressing the challenges of autonomous vehicles such as safety, communication and interoperability. Internet of Things – IEEE standards are enabling the seamless integration of billions of connected devices. Smart homes, smart fridges – they all partake in the IoT revolution that is largely defined and guided by the IEEE. The IEEE plays a vital role in shaping the technology landscape by developing and promoting standards that drive innovation, ensure interoperability and enhance safety. From the devices that we use every day to the infrastructure that powers our world, IEEE standards are making a positive impact on our lives. With the Association for Computer Machinery or the ACM, they are one of the two most important institutions in providing accessible information in the domain that we are interested in to vast communities of people.

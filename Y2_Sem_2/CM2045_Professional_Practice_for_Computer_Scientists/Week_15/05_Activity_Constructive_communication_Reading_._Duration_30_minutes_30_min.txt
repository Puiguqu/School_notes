Linus Torvalds is recognized as the creator of the Linux kernel. This kernel constitutes the core operating system foundation of Linux. He is also credited with developing Git, the widely used version control software. Historically, however, Torvalds was known for an aggressive communication style. This history of behavior has subsequently been addressed by him. Torvalds issued a public apology regarding these past "unprofessional" rants. This apology was documented in The Register (2025). Specifically, the article is titled: Williams C. ‘Linux kernel’s Torvalds: ‘I am truly sorry’ for my ‘unprofessional’ rants, I need a break to get help’. Following this apology, he committed to substantial behavioral improvements. He pledged to foster a significantly more inclusive and respectful environment within the developer community. This commitment includes active support for a new code of conduct for Linux developers. The current activity centers on reviewing a specific dataset. This dataset comprises examples of Linus Torvalds’ past 'rants.' The primary task requires the student to select one specific rant from the dataset. The goal is to rewrite this chosen text in a constructive and professional manner. It must be noted that the original messages contain harsh or profane language. These originals may be highly offensive or uncomfortable for the reader. These examples are provided strictly as learning and practice opportunities. They are designed to teach the skills of professional communication. The guidelines offer several tips for de-escalating the tone. One tip is to replace harsh language with neutral, professional terms. Instead of judgmental statements, one should use measured concerns, such as saying, 'I have some concerns about this approach.' Critically, the focus must shift from criticizing the person to critiquing the problem itself. Instead of accusing someone of incompetence, one should explain the specific issue and its resulting impact. A key guideline is to always offer potential solutions or helpful suggestions. Criticism should never stand alone; it must be paired with constructive alternatives. Furthermore, the use of 'I' statements is highly recommended. This allows the communicator to express personal feelings without direct accusation. Maintaining a respectful and collaborative tone is paramount in all communication. Even when strongly disagreeing, the tone must encourage understanding. Finally, students must also report any broken links found within the page via the Student Portal.

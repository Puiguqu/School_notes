Let's delve into its origins, principles and applications. Kanban's roots are in manufacturing. It emerged from Toyota's innovative production system in the 1940s, inspired by how supermarkets stocked shelves. What we historically refer to as lean manufacturing. Think about a production line, optimized for purpose, prioritizing key steps and stages. Delivering value in what people want over obscure, nuanced features and things. The just-in-time inventory. Kanban boards with their columns and cards provide a clear picture of work items and their progress. They are also designed in such a way to limit the amount of work you do at any one time. Kanban principles include the following. Visualize the workflow. Make all work items and their status visible to the entire team. Who is working on what? When, i.e. now.

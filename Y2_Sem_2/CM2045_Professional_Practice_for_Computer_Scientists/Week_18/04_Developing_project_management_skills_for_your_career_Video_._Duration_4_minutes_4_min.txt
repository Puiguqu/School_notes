# Developing project management skills for your career

The University of London Career Service offers micro-modules designed to enhance student project management skills alongside academic study. These resources are available via the University of London virtual learning environment and are crucial for developing career insights. The service provides several micro-modules: Global Employability Skills, Professional Impact Profile and Success, and Career Planning. All three modules were professionally developed by experts at the University of London Career Service. Upon completion of each module, students receive a certificate recognized on their degree transcript. The "Professional Impact Profile and Success" micro-module is the primary resource covering project management skills. This module is highly structured into four specialized strands: the persuasive professional, the strategic professional, the entrepreneurial professional, and the market-aware professional. The dedicated focus on project management skills resides within the entrepreneurial professional strand. This section covers the three distinct phases of the project lifecycle. Students learn that project management skills can be developed outside professional work. For example, major domestic tasks like childcare or home improvements provide valuable practice in planning and problem-solving. Furthermore, volunteering on community projects offers a less competitive environment to learn project management approaches from others. Academic life itself provides opportunities for skill development, requiring students to apply project management approaches to improve study effectiveness. The content also advises on demonstrating these skills in CVs, applications, or interviews, such as balancing academic and professional deadlines through careful prioritization. Other modules also contribute significantly; the persuasive professional strand offers advice on negotiating, and the strategic professional strand explores time management. The curriculum also features advanced dedicated training. The coursework schedule spans 20 weeks, addressing diverse skills from Weeks 1 and 2, which cover Written Communication Skills. Weeks 3 and 4 focus on Presentation and Public Speaking Skills. Weeks 5 and 6 address Legal and Ethical Foundations, while Weeks 7 and 8 cover Inclusive Computing and Ethical Impacts. Sustainability in Computing is covered across Weeks 9 and 10, followed by Risk Management and Security in Weeks 11 and 12. Weeks 13 and 14 focus on Industry Standards and Best Practices. The final major skills training occurs in Weeks 15 and 16, which address Teamwork and Collaboration. Project management itself is covered extensively across Weeks 17 and 18. Notably, Week 18 includes dedicated lessons, such as "Agile project management - Case study" and "Tools for Agile project management." The curriculum concludes with professional development and career planning in Week 19 and Week 20, solidifying the goal of career readiness.

This lecture transcends specific software tools to explore fundamental agile project management techniques applicable to any aspiring computer scientist. Whether you're organising conferences, conducting research or building the next revolutionary app, these techniques will empower you to navigate the complexities of project delivery with confidence and efficiency. So why do techniques matter? Tools are transient. The popular project management app of today may be obsolete tomorrow. Techniques however remain timeless and adaptable. Universality. Whether you're using a physical whiteboard or sophisticated software, understanding core techniques allows you to adapt to any situation and toolset. Foundations for success. Techniques provide the underlying framework for effective planning, execution and monitoring of projects, irrespective of their specific nature. Let's start with visualising the workflow and using Kanban in action. Beyond the digital, Kanban can be implemented with a simple whiteboard, sticky notes and markers, offering a tangible and accessible approach to workflow visualisation. Mapping the flow. Physically representing the stages of your project, to do, in progress and done, and the movement of tasks through each of these stages. Limiting the work in progress. Constrain the number of tasks in progress by physically limiting the space allocated to each stage, promoting focus and flow. The benefits of this tool include enhanced visibility, reduced work in progress and improved collaboration through a shared understanding of the project status. Now let's move on to sprint planning, templates and techniques. Sprint planning templates offer a structured framework for defining sprint goals, selecting tasks and estimating effort, ensuring a shared understanding within the team. The key elements of this framework include a sprint goal, a concise and clear statement of what the team aims to achieve during the sprint. A sprint backlog, a prioritised list of tasks selected from the product backlog, guiding the team's focus. Task breakdown, decomposing user stories into smaller, more manageable tasks to facilitate progress tracking. Effort estimation, employing techniques like story points or time estimates to gauge the workload and ensure realistic sprint planning. The benefits of sprint planning include improved focus, shared understanding and realistic planning, leading to increased team efficiency and successful sprint completion. Automation and using Selenium for efficiency. Selenium, while primarily known for automating web browser testing, can also be leveraged to automate repetitive tasks within the project management process itself. Examples of this are generating reports, automate the creation of progress reports, burn down charts and other project metrics, saving time and ensuring accuracy. Updating task status, automatically update task status based on code commits, test results or other relevant triggers, maintaining a real-time view of progress. Sending notifications, automate reminders and notifications to keep the team informed and ensure timely completion of tasks. This results in increased efficiency, reduced manual effort and improved accuracy. Freeing up time for more strategic project management activities. Risk management, including identifying and mitigating risks. Even seemingly small projects carry inherent risks. Identifying and addressing these proactively is crucial for successful project delivery. You can do this through a simple spreadsheet highlighting things like the nature of the risk, its likelihood of occurring from low to high, its impact from low to high and so on. Techniques. Risk identification. Conduct brainstorming sessions to identify potential risks and document them systematically. Risk assessment. Evaluate the likelihood and potential impact of each identified risk to prioritise mitigation efforts. Risk mitigation. Develop and implement strategies to reduce the likelihood or minimise the impact of potential risks. Risk monitoring. Continuously monitor identified risks and adjust mitigation strategies as needed throughout the project lifecycle. This will reduce surprises, improve project outcomes and increase confidence in achieving project goals. Communication channels and choosing the right medium are also important. While email remains essential, it's not always the most efficient or effective communication channel for project management. If it's time sensitive or vague, I'll often rely on a quick Teams message to get my point across. This can also be helpful to talk while someone is presenting, almost a second thread of ideas that people can come back to retrospectively. I find this helpful for remote and in-person hybrid events. Consider matching the message to the medium. Instant messaging, ideal for quick questions, informal updates and real-time collaboration. For example, Slack or Microsoft Teams. Video conferencing facilitates richer communication for complex discussions, brainstorming sessions and team building. For example, Zoom. Our webinars were a good example of this, supporting anonymous questions and answers, chat, quiz questions and the like. Project wikis provide a central repository for documentation, knowledge sharing and project updates, e.g. confluence or notion. Face-to-face meetings, reserved for critical discussions, decision making and relationship building. Agile does advocate for this and it's one of the contentious points I'm not so sure about. Finally, meeting management and making the most of time. Meetings are essential, but they can also be time consuming and unproductive if not managed effectively. Techniques for effective meetings include having clear objectives, define the purpose and desired outcomes of the meeting beforehand. Prepared agendas. Circulate agendas in advance to ensure everyone is prepared. Make sure the key points of contention are transparent. Time boxing. Allocate specific time slots for each agenda item to keep the meeting focused. Active participation. Encourage all participants to contribute and share their perspectives. Concise minutes. Document key decisions and action items for future reference. No reason to repeat things with good minutes. This should help improve decision making, increased efficiency and enhance team engagement. Project management techniques are indispensable skills for any computer scientist, irrespective of the specific tools employed. By mastering techniques like Kanban, sprint planning, automation and risk management, you can effectively plan, execute and monitor projects of any scale and complexity.

# Activity: Updating your LinkedIn profile
Reading•
. Duration: 30 minutes
30 min - Summary

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-cm2045-professional-practice-for-computer-scientists/supplement/6YQ8c/activity-updating-your-linkedin-profile

1. Creating a LinkedIn profile is highly recommended if one is not already established.
2. Users must remain aware that all profile usage is subject to LinkedIn’s specific terms and conditions and privacy policy.
3. The initial profile photo selection must be a high-quality, professional image that conveys a neat and friendly look, without requiring a formal suit and tie.
4. The background for the profile photo should be simple and well-lit for maximum professionalism.
5. The headline is critical and must include the current or target job title alongside several relevant keywords.
6. For example, instead of a generic title like ‘Computer Science student,’ one should use a detailed phrase such as ‘Aspiring Software Engineer | Python | AI Enthusiast.’
7. The About section should begin with a brief introduction outlining one's passion for computer science, technical skills, academic projects, and career goals.
8. This section needs to be keyword-optimized for target roles, mentioning technical skills like Python and C++, alongside global employability skills such as collaboration and problem solving.
9. Crucially, the 'About' section must be kept succinct, as only approximately 400 characters will display automatically.
10. The Experience section requires listing relevant work history, encompassing jobs, internships, and freelance contributions.
11. It is also essential to include academic projects and volunteer activities, such as mentoring at a coding bootcamp or contributing to open-source projects, detailing the technologies used and challenges overcome.
12. When describing experience, focus rigorously on quantifiable results, stating how a process was improved, a problem was solved, or how a team project was positively influenced.
13. The Education section must showcase the University of London BSc Computer Science degree, including the expected graduation year and any honors received.
14. Furthermore, include specific courses aligned with career objectives, such as Algorithms, Data Structures, or Artificial Intelligence.
15. The Skills section requires listing key programming languages, tools, and concepts relevant to the target job market.
16. To validate abilities, users should seek endorsements from colleagues and classmates, and take formal LinkedIn Skills Assessments for verification, citing examples like Python, Java, or SQL.
17. Additional professional credibility can be added by including relevant certifications such as AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner, Microsoft Azure Fundamentals, or coursework from Coursera/edX.
18. The featured section is ideal for highlighting standout achievements, including major projects, relevant articles, or professional portfolios.
19. Users should attach tangible evidence, such as GitHub repositories or direct links to live projects, within this featured area.
20. To improve digital identity, a custom URL should be built and edited to something professional, adhering to the format like linkedin.com/in/yourname.
21. Maintaining a consistent presence is vital, requiring the regular updating of the profile with new skills, experiences, or accomplishments to keep the profile fresh and relevant.
22. If the individual does not yet have a profile, the immediate action is to create one to establish a professional digital presence.
23. The profile structure must effectively merge academic excellence (University of London degree) with practical application (GitHub links).
24. Attention to detail is required in ensuring all listed technical skills are directly applicable to the sought-after industry roles.
25. By systematically implementing these technical and content best practices, the profile becomes a robust professional marketing tool.

# Review your GitHub or LinkedIn profile
Reading•
. Duration: 30 minutes
30 min - Summary

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-cm2045-professional-practice-for-computer-scientists/supplement/nzexA/review-your-github-or-linkedin-profile

Both GitHub and LinkedIn serve as crucial platforms for engaging with and leveraging your professional community. To maximize the utility of these tools, it is highly beneficial to conduct a thorough review or discussion of your profile with your peers. This activity can be carried out either as a self-reflection exercise or as a formal peer review activity. If coordinating a swap for feedback, utilizing the dedicated discussion forum is recommended. The text provides detailed checklists to guide a quick audit of both GitHub and LinkedIn profiles.

For a comprehensive review of your GitHub profile, the first key element is completeness. Your profile must feature a professional photo, a detailed bio, and accurate contact information. Furthermore, users should pin their best projects directly to the profile for immediate visibility. All associated README files must be clear, highly informative, and demonstrably well-written. A skilled profile should clearly demonstrate active contribution to various projects and engagement within the broader community. Evidence of open-source contributions that align specifically with your professional interests is strongly encouraged. Technically, show proficiency by demonstrating the effective use of version control concepts, such as branching, merging, and pull requests. Beyond coding, highlight efforts where you have improved documentation, authored tutorials, or translated content.

Regarding the professional profile on LinkedIn, the criteria for completeness are rigorous. The profile must be 100% complete, encompassing a professional photo, a descriptive headline, a summary, detailed education section, and comprehensive work experience. The headline must strategically highlight both your specialized professional interests and core expertise. Your summary section should be personalized and succinctly showcase your skills, key achievements, and overarching career goals. When describing past roles, include detailed descriptions of responsibilities and specific accomplishments. It is also important to include relevant academic projects and any volunteer work performed. Ensure you list relevant skills and feature endorsements received from both colleagues and professional connections. Finally, include full degree studies, other specialized certifications, and any relevant coursework or projects to build a robust profile. For professional polish and easier sharing, always create a custom LinkedIn URL.

# Activity: Updating your LinkedIn profile
Reading•
. Duration: 30 minutes
30 min

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-cm2045-professional-practice-for-computer-scientists/supplement/6YQ8c/activity-updating-your-linkedin-profile

We encourage you to create a LinkedIn profile if you do not already have one. Please note that you will be subject to LinkedIn’s terms and conditions and privacy policy. Use the following checklist to enhance your LinkedIn profile. Profile photo: Choose a high-quality, professional photo. Aim for a neat, friendly look (no need for a suit and tie). Keep the background simple and well-lit Headline: Include your current role or target job title and a few keywords. Example: Instead of ‘Computer Science student,’ use ‘Aspiring Software Engineer | Python | AI Enthusiast.’ About section: Begin with a brief introduction to who you are and what excites you about computer science. your technical skills, academic projects and career goals. Use keywords relevant to the roles you’re targeting. Technical skills such as Python, C++ and Global Employability Skills such as collaboration and communication and problem solving. Keep it succinct (around 400 characters will display automatically). Experience section: List relevant jobs, internships and freelance work experience. Include academic projects, describing the technologies used and challenges overcome. Include volunteer activities, especially those related to tech, like mentoring at a coding bootcamp or contributing to open-source projects Focus on results—mention how you improved a process, solved a problem or contributed to a team project. Highlight your role, accomplishments and the impact of your work. Education section: Showcase your University of London BSc Computer Science degree, including your expected graduation year and any honors or distinctions Include courses that align with your career goals, like Algorithms, Data Structures or Artificial Intelligence Skills section: List key programming languages, tools and concepts relevant to your target jobs. Ask classmates or colleagues to endorse your skills. Take LinkedIn Skills Assessments to verify your skills (e.g., Python, Java, SQL). Add certifications: Include relevant certifications, like AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner, Microsoft Azure Fundamentals, or Coursera/edX courses Use the featured section: Highlight standout projects, articles or portfolios. Attach GitHub repositories or links to live projects Build a custom URL: Edit your profile URL to something professional, (e.g., linkedin.com/in/yourname). Stay active: Regularly update your profile with new skills, experiences or achievements to keep it fresh and relevant.

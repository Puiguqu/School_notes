# Review your GitHub or LinkedIn profile
Reading•
. Duration: 30 minutes
30 min

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-cm2045-professional-practice-for-computer-scientists/supplement/nzexA/review-your-github-or-linkedin-profile

Both GitHub and LinkedIn give you the opportunity to engage with your professional community. Taking steps to review or discuss your profile with your peers can help you to make the most of these tools. You can carry out the following activity as a self-reflection activity or as a peer review activity. If you would like to swap profiles with a peer to give feedback, use the discussion forum to find someone to work with on this. Use these checklists to do a quick audit of your (your peers') GitHub or LinkedIn profile. Questions to help you review your GitHub profile Is your profile complete with a professional photo, bio and contact information? Have you pinned your best projects to your profile? Are your READMEs clear, informative and well-written? Can the reader see that you are contributing to projects and engaging with the community? Is there evidence that you have contributed to open-source projects that align with your interests? Are you showing that you effectively use branching, merging and pull requests? Do you show how you have improved documentation, written tutorials or translated content? Questions to help you review your LinkedIn profile Is your profile 100% complete with a professional photo, headline, summary and detailed education, and work experience? Does your headline highlight your professional interests and expertise Does your summary succinctly showcase your skills, achievements and career goals? Is it personalised? Have you included detailed descriptions of your roles, responsibilities and accomplishments? Have you included relevant academic projects and volunteering? Have you listed relevant skills and endorsements received from colleagues and connections? Have you included your degree study, other certifications and relevant coursework or projects? Have you created a custom LinkedIn URL to make your profile easier to share and more professional?

1. The University of London Career Service, via Senior Careers Consultant Liz Wilkinson, outlines how mastering communication skills is vital for job placement and long-term career development. 2. The core focus is on assertive communication skills, both written and spoken, which significantly enhance every phase of the job search process. 3. Effective assertive communication ensures more persuasive interview performance, increases the likelihood of being shortlisted, and equips job holders to negotiate strong salaries. 4. Employers and recruiters highly value communication because skilled communicators improve organizational viability, facilitate teamwork, resolve conflicts, and manage stakeholder relationships. 5. Assertive communication is defined as the necessary middle ground between passive and overly aggressive communication styles. 6. Assertive communication contains three fundamental elements: understanding the audience's priorities, possessing relevant facts and evidence, and presenting a clear line of argument with a positive suggestion for next steps. 7. Communication is designated as one of the 10 global employability skills identified by the Career Service, linking closely with persuasion and negotiation—the "human advantage" in an AI-enriched world. 8. While AI tools can improve clarity, grammar, and CV targeting, the unique human ability to link personal experience to recruiter needs remains indispensable. 9. A common recruiter complaint is the volume of bland, over-generalized CVs, emphasizing the need for personalized human touch. 10. When improving a CV or application, applicants must adopt the recruiter’s perspective, addressing the potential concerns about retention, deadlines, and team fit. 11. To powerfully back up statements, applicants should demonstrate the *impact* and *results* of their skills, encompassing technical and global employability competencies. 12. For structuring application statements, the **STAR** approach is recommended: **S**ituation (description of the context), **T**ask (the goal to be achieved), **A**ctions (the specific steps taken, requiring strong verbs), and **R**esults (the positive business impact). 13. The second 'R' in the application process should serve as a reflection, detailing what was learned and what would be done differently. 14. The entire CV or application functions as an argument, asserting that the candidate’s skills will make them impactful in that specific role and environment. 15. The resources available include the global employability skills micro-module, which offers a dedicated section on technical communication skills. 16. **Formulae/Technique Highlight:** The **STARR** technique is also applicable for practice assignments (Application of STARR technique).

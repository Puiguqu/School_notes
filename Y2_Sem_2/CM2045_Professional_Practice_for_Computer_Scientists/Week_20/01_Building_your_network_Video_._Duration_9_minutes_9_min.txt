Building a strong professional network is vital for technology professionals, providing mentorship, advice, and potential job leads. These connections allow access to unadvertised roles, as employers value referrals for their reliability and trust. A successful network indicates a candidate possesses both technical skills and the interpersonal ability to thrive in team environments. The fundamental concept of networking is built on mutual value, moving beyond simply asking what others can do for you. Instead, networking operates as a cycle of reciprocity, where helping others strengthens relationships for future benefit. To expand your network, focus on finding a common link, such as a shared academic program or the wider University of London connection. Mutual acquaintances are also key, utilizing your existing professional, family, or social circles for introductions. Beyond immediate contacts, attending industry events—whether virtual or physical—is an excellent opportunity to meet new professionals. Specific venues like conferences, hackathons, and discussions serve as ideal settings for gaining first-hand job information. Global online platforms such as GitHub, LinkedIn, X, and online discussion boards are excellent tools for building contacts and asking questions. When engaging at events, always prepare by conducting homework and researching the people you intend to meet. You must be ready to present your objectives and begin conversations by asking open-ended questions. For online events, actively engage in the chat box, and use person-to-person messaging to exchange contact details. After meeting someone, follow up by sending a courtesy email thanking them and referencing specific details from your conversation. Always keep your contacts informed of your progress, particularly if it relates to advice they provided. Crucially, networking requires consistently providing value to maintain the relationship, such as sharing articles or congratulating them on achievements. Maintaining professional relationships demands genuine interest and continuous effort to collaborate. This process develops global employability skills, including adaptability, collaboration, and clear communication. Furthermore, networking helps develop complex skills like emotional intelligence, problem-solving, and leadership. For career planning, the University of London Career Service offers resources and webinars to build both regional and global networks. These resources include connecting with the global LinkedIn page and leveraging the University of London Alumni Ambassador Network. Start building your network today to enhance your employability and expand your professional impact across all career stages.

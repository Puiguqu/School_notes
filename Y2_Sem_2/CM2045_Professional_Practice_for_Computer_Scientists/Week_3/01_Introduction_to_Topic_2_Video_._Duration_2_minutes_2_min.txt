This material introduces Topic 2: Public Speaking and Presentation Skills, a crucial area for both professional and academic advancement. The objective is to teach students how to build the confidence required to deliver engaging presentations. Participants will learn fundamental elements of strong public speaking, including voice control, body language, and maintaining eye contact. A primary goal is mastering the ability to manage performance nervousness and transform it into positive energy. The content emphasizes crafting structured, engaging presentation content that can be tailored for diverse audiences. Whether explaining technical concepts or pitching ideas, effective communication is highlighted as the key skill. The course's pedagogical framework employs the CAFE model, which helps maintain focus and prevents the lecturer from going off on irrelevant tangents. Improvement is facilitated through quizzes, reflective discussions, and analysis of real-world examples. By the conclusion of this topic, students will be able not only to deliver powerful speeches but also to engage and successfully persuade any audience.

The subsequent curriculum outlines a detailed 20-week learning journey covering professional development. Week 1 and Week 2 focus specifically on Written Communication Skills, split into two parts. Week 3, Presentation and Public Speaking Skills Part 1, is intensive, featuring a 15-minute "How do you feel about public speaking?" discussion prompt. This week also includes an activity reflecting on presentations and practice assignments, totaling significant practical time. The following weeks build upon presentation skills, with Week 4 dedicated to Public Speaking Skills Part 2. From Week 5 to Week 6, the curriculum shifts to Legal and Ethical Foundations, covering both parts. Weeks 7 and 8 address Inclusive Computing and its ethical impacts. Weeks 9 and 10 cover Sustainability in Computing, divided into two modules. The focus then moves to technical skills in Weeks 11 and 12, covering Risk Management and Security. Weeks 13 and 14 tackle Industry Standards and Best Practices, divided into two parts. The curriculum progresses to soft skills with Week 15 and Week 16, focusing on Teamwork and Collaboration. Project Management is studied over Week 17 and Week 18, requiring dedicated attention. The final two weeks, Week 19 and Week 20, are dedicated to Professional Development and Career Planning, completing the comprehensive learning path.

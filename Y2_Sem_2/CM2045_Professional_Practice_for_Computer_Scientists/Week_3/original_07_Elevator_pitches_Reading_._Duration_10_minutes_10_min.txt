Elevator pitches are important because they help you quickly and effectively communicate your ideas, whether you're explaining a project, pitching a product or sharing your skills. In just a few sentences, you need to capture attention, create interest and leave a memorable impression – skills that are valuable in networking, job interviews and professional conversations. The two videos the follow are examples of elevator pitches – one that follows best practice and one that doesn't. Watch the two videos and then reflect on what you did and didn't like about them. Create a table of 'dos and don'ts' for elevator pitches based on your reflection. This will be useful for you as later in this lesson we will be asking you to create your own elevator pitch. After you've watched both videos, you can check your answers against our model answers.

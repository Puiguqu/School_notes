What did you think of those pitches? 'Elevator pitch 2' was designed to follow best practice. Though there was lots of enthusiasm in 'Elevator pitch 1', it was too long and not focused enough to succeed as an elevator pitch. You can read our more in-depth feedback below. Elevator pitch 1 Elevator pitch 2 Overly verbose and too long: includes long, overly complex explanations that lose the listener's attention. Example: 'Machine learning, which is basically a kind of artificial intelligence—though not quite the same, because machine learning is about, um, developing models that can learn from data without explicitly being programmed—is really interesting in healthcare.' Clarity and simplicity: Uses accessible language to explain a complex topic. Example: 'My work focuses on developing algorithms that analyse medical imaging data, such as X-rays and MRIs, to help doctors diagnose diseases earlier and more reliably.' Excessive scientific jargon: Uses technical language without clear purpose or relevance, confusing the listener. Example: 'MRIs use strong magnetic fields and radio waves to create highly detailed images of internal organs.' Concrete evidence: Provides empirical data to strengthen credibility. Example: 'Studies show that AI-driven diagnostic tools can improve accuracy by up to 20% in detecting conditions such as cancer or cardiovascular disease.' Unfocused content: Talks about different aspects without a clear point or benefit, making it hard to follow. Example: 'For instance, we’re working with MRI scans, which, if you’re not aware, stands for magnetic resonance imaging.' Specificity and focus: Focuses on the main point without unnecessary details, keeping the listener’s attention on the key benefits. Example: 'This technology enhances patient outcomes by catching diseases earlier and reduces costs, with one study estimating AI could save the healthcare industry over £100 billion annually.' Unclear purpose or impact: Lacks a clear description of the potential benefits or real-world impact. Example: Ends with: 'So, yeah, we’re using machine learning, and it has a lot of potential… I think.' Logical flow: Clearly organised structure that moves smoothly from problem to solution to impact. Confusing and disorganised structure: Fails to follow a logical flow from problem to solution to impact, resulting in a muddled message.

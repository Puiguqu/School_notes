# Improving your presentation skills to create more effective interview and assessment centre performances

1. Mastering presentation skills is crucial for computer science students aiming to excel in technology sector recruitment scenarios.
2. Effective presentation abilities are vital for delivering persuasive talks to both technical and non-technical audiences, which is key to securing a role.
3. These skills enhance performance at every job hunting stage, from standard interviews to complex assessment centres and group tasks.
4. Strong presentation skills allow candidates to showcase both their technical expertise and crucial interpersonal abilities to recruiters.
5. Employers highly value these skills because they demonstrate clear communication, teamwork potential, and professionalism.
6. In technology, where complex concepts must reach diverse stakeholders, clarity and confidence in presenting are especially prized.
7. The core to success involves mastering not only the content but also the delivery, requiring audience engagement.
8. Techniques for effective delivery include clear structuring, emphasizing key points, and using visuals to enhance understanding.
9. Practicing non-verbal cues—such as eye contact, posture, and gestures—helps establish a stronger and more credible connection with the audience.
10. When tailoring a presentation, candidates must adapt their message, avoiding technical jargon unless the audience understands it.
11. Instead of jargon, one should use analogies or visual aids to explain complicated ideas and always spell out acronyms.
12. Visual aids must remain clear and data-focused, specifically highlighting how the information impacts the company or project goals.
13. Good presentation skills are underpinned by the University of London's global employability skills, including clear communication.
14. Emotional intelligence is necessary because presenting requires considering what matters to the audience and anticipating their reactions.
15. Collaboration skills are enhanced by rehearsing presentations and approaches with friends or mentors.
16. Artificial Intelligence (AI) serves as a powerful tool, assisting with grammar checks and enhancing message clarity.
17. AI-driven platforms, such as ChatGBT, can generate content ideas or facilitate practice question-and-answer sessions.
18. Tools like Microsoft PowerPoint have built-in rehearsal coaches, offering assistance with timing and pacing.
19. Critically, AI must only assist preparation; human intelligence is required to ensure the presentation is properly targeted to the audience level.
20. The approach must be highly human, engaging, and interactive to make a lasting positive impression.
21. Presentation strategy must be adapted based on the career stage: starters focus on confidence, developers on refinement, and changers on transferable skills.
22. Following a short presentation, candidates must be prepared for a Q&A session, using questions as chances to showcase complex problem-solving.
23. If an answer is unknown, the candidate should be honest and propose how they would investigate the information.
24. Candidates can utilize specialist digital tools provided by the University of London Career Service for personalized feedback on interview performance.
25. Ultimately, consistent practice, careful design, and self-reflection are mandatory for maximizing interview and assessment centre performance.

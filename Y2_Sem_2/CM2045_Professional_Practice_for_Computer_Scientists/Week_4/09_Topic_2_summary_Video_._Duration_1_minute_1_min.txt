The course provided a comprehensive overview of effective public speaking and presentation skills. Key concepts covered include managing one's voice, controlling body language, and maintaining unwavering confidence during presentations. Techniques were taught to manage nervousness and project poise, even in high-pressure professional scenarios. Structuring content is identified as crucial, ensuring presentations are both engaging and clearly convey key messages. Participants learned how to tailor their presentation material effectively for diverse audiences, whether technical or general. Furthermore, the curriculum emphasized the ability to evaluate and improve personal presentation techniques through analysis, reflection, and quizzes. Beyond theory, these public speaking skills are presented as vital for career advancement in today's job market. According to the careers team, mastering this skill set provides a significant competitive edge, whether pitching ideas or answering challenging interview questions. Ultimately, effective communication is necessary not only for giving presentations but for making a strong professional impression and building a robust professional brand.

The associated curriculum structure spans 20 weeks of development, commencing with written communication skills. Weeks 1 and 2 focus on "Written communication skills, Part 1" and "Part 2," respectively. Presentation and public speaking skills are detailed in Weeks 3 and 4, split into "Part 1" and "Part 2." Lesson 4.1 specifically covers "Visual aids and technology - Presentation zen – using visual aids in presentations," with associated modules on practice assignments and handling audience questions. Following this, Lesson 4.2 offers "Career development insights from the University of London Careers Service," focusing on enhancing interview performance and presentation skills. The curriculum continues through Week 5, dedicated to "Legal and ethical foundations, Part 1." Weeks 6, 7, and 8 address "Legal and ethical foundations, Part 2," and "Inclusive computing and ethical impacts, Part 1" and "Part 2." Subsequent learning modules build upon broader professional competency. Week 9 focuses on "Sustainability in computing, Part 1," followed by Week 10 on "Sustainability in computing, Part 2." Weeks 11 and 12 tackle "Risk management and security," comprising "Part 1" and "Part 2" modules. The instruction progresses through Week 13 and Week 14, covering "Industry standards and best practices, Part 1" and "Part 2." Later modules address teamwork and project management, with Week 15 and Week 16 dedicated to "Teamwork and collaboration, Part 1" and "Part 2." Finally, the course concludes with Weeks 17, 18, 19, and 20, covering "Project management," and "Professional development and career planning," specifically "Part 1" and "Part 2" respectively.

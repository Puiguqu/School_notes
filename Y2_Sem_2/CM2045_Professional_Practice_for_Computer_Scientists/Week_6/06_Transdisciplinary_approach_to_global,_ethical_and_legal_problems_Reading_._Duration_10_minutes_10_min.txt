# Transdisciplinary approach to global, ethical and legal problems

1. The current technology operating environment is fast-changing, driven particularly by the game-changing impact of AI.
2. This evolution introduces a continuous stream of new ethical and legal dilemmas that organizations must navigate to maintain viability.
3. These ethical and legal dilemmas are inherently complex and therefore require a broad range of perspectives for effective resolution.
4. A specialized approach, described as transdisciplinary working, is necessary to address such complexity.
5. Transdisciplinary working involves bringing together professionals who can share viewpoints from multiple technical and non-technical disciplines.
6. Such collaborative efforts are highly effective for solving complex problems together.
7. Transdisciplinary human teams represent an excellent method for leveraging new technologies to drive organizational strategy.
8. Their utilization results in enhanced and creative solutions to tricky dilemmas.
9. Furthermore, these teams are significantly less prone to the pitfalls of group think and blind spots in problem articulation and solution.
10. They are also more likely to accurately surface the impact on a fuller range of stakeholders and users of any proposed solution.
11. As a result, employers in the tech sector are increasingly prioritizing and looking for transdisciplinary working skills.
12. These skills are sought after in both new recruits and aspiring leaders within the industry.
13. Candidates must demonstrate the ability to listen effectively to others.
14. It is crucial to identify the professional or cultural lenses or biases that both oneself and colleagues may bring to a situation.
15. A key skill is the ability to work effectively with colleagues who hold very different perspectives when facing dilemmas.
16. To practice these transdisciplinary skills, one can utilize the regular University of London Careers Service webinars.
17. These webinars specifically focus on collaborative problem solving and transdisciplinary thinking.
18. A concrete example of this practice is the sample webinar titled, "The Impact of AI on Tech Roles."
19. When listening to this sample webinar, one should take a transdisciplinary approach by considering multiple lenses.
20. These lenses include the Academic lens (which technology specialism interests you most?).
21. Another necessary perspective is the Lived experience lens (where are you now and what is your previous experience?).
22. Additionally, the Career stage lens must be considered (how much work experience do you have; are you a career starter, developer, or changer?).
23. These different perspectives can dramatically affect how one views ethical and legal dilemmas in a professional context.

# Topic 3 summary

. Duration: 15 minutes

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-cm2045-professional-practice-for-computer-scientists/supplement/PeL1T/topic-3-summary

In this topic, we explored the legal and ethical considerations that shape computing, starting with the legal and ethical foundations of the field. We examined the key principles that govern professional responsibilities, distinguishing between what is legally permissible and what is ethically responsible. Legal obligations, such as intellectual property laws, were also considered in relation to their effect on the computing industry. This provided a basis for understanding the broader impact of legal and ethical constraints in computing. Building on this, we delved into ethical decision-making and dilemmas, analysing real-world scenarios where computing professionals must navigate complex choices. We talked briefly about the ethical minefield of running a study ‘in the wild’ and the process of designing and developing with ethics in mind. Finally, we examined sustainability and ethics, focusing on the impact of technology on society and the environment. We addressed issues such as inclusivity and accessibility, emphasising the importance of designing tools and services that cater to diverse users. Through case studies and discussions, we evaluated the ethical challenges in computing and explored ways to align legal compliance with ethical best practices, ensuring responsible and sustainable technological development.

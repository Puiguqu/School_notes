# What is Studiosity?

. Duration: 10 minutes

The University of London is currently piloting a specialized online service called Studiosity. This innovative platform is designed specifically to support students with their academic writing needs. Studiosity extends its utility far beyond mere writing assistance. It also provides additional academic support for topics requiring a refresher during a student's study tenure. For instance, basic mathematical questions can be addressed through the service. Users can upload various types of drafts, including essays, reports, and other written assessments. Once uploaded, experts in academic writing will thoroughly review the material. These experts provide detailed and constructive feedback, explaining precisely how the student's work can be improved. Students can access Studiosity to learn more about how the service functions. A primary usage method involves uploading an assignment draft to receive fast feedback. This process helps students verify they are correctly on track with their foundational work. Furthermore, the service allows users to chat live for instant guidance. This live chat feature covers feedback on writing assignments. It also handles questions related to mathematics, science, or general study topics. Students utilize this resource for specific structural concerns. For example, a student might ask if an assignment needs structuring as a formal report. Another concern involves questioning the logical sense of a conclusion. Students can also seek feedback regarding the coherence of multiple, distinct arguments within a paper. Moreover, the platform addresses specific technical queries, such as troubleshooting why a calculated answer for 'x' is incorrect. These advisory services confirm that even if the preliminary working appears correct, assistance is available. All these types of queries represent the core areas where Studiosity advisors can effectively assist. Studiosity serves as a comprehensive resource for academic excellence. Its goal is to guide students toward improving their academic output and understanding.

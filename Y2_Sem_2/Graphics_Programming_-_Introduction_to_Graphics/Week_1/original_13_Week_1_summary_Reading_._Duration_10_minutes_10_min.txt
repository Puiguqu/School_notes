In week 1 we introduced the fundamentals of 2D transformations in computer graphics, essential for creating and manipulating objects within a two-dimensional space. You have explored how transformations such as translation, rotation, and scaling work, gaining an understanding of how they alter objects' positions, orientations, and sizes on a digital canvas. In addition to learning about transformations, you have also worked with classes, which are fundamental to organizing and managing complex graphics. By using classes, you have streamlined the process of creating and animating graphic elements. Practical applications included programming a basic clock and a simplified model of the solar system. Through these projects, you have applied transformations to create animations, like rotating hands on a clock and moving planets around the sun.

By the end of this week, you should be able to explain how transformations function, understand the role of classes in organizing graphics, and use these concepts to create animated 2D models. As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. 1. Understanding of 2D Transformations I can explain what 2D transformations are and how they are used in computer graphics. I understand the difference between translation, rotation, and scaling transformations. I can identify real-world applications of 2D transformations, such as creating animations. 2. Application of 2D Transformations I have used translation to move objects within a 2D space. I have used rotation to change the orientation of objects. I have applied scaling to alter the size of objects. I successfully used transformations to program a basic clock with moving hands. I applied transformations to program a simple model of a solar system with orbiting planets. 3. Understanding and use of classes I understand what classes are and their importance in organizing graphics code. I can create classes to represent objects in a graphic program. I have used classes to manage multiple objects in my projects, like planets in the solar system model. 4. Overall learning outcomes I can confidently explain how transformations work in 2D graphics. I can describe the role of classes in organizing and managing graphical elements. I can independently use transformations and classes to program animated 2D models. 6. Reflection Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Week 1. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge.

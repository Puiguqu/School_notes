This text comprises two distinct parts: an instructional guide for the Studiosity service and a detailed, structured academic syllabus spanning 20 weeks. Studiosity is presented as an essential tool for students, helping them achieve confidence and academic success by integrating it into their regular study routine. To utilize the service, students must log into the institutional student portal and locate the designated Studiosity link. Once inside the account, the page should be bookmarked for easy subsequent access. The service offers two primary methods of assistance: document feedback and specialist consultation. For written drafts, whether an essay, report, or assignment, users must click "submit your document" and follow the established prompts. Students commonly upload their drafts late at night to receive feedback the following morning. Following review by a specialist, users will receive both an email and a mobile notification, allowing them to view feedback and make necessary revisions before the final submission. Alternatively, if a student has a study question or requires instant writing feedback, they can select "connect now." This feature connects the user to the next available specialist, who can assist with various subjects, including writing, English skills, concepts, math, science, and essential skills. Thousands of students globally are reportedly using this service to feel more confident in their writing and studies. The accompanying syllabus outlines a 20-week academic program with specific technical and conceptual focus areas. Early weeks cover foundational topics, including Graphics in Week 1 and Vectors and animation in Week 2. Subsequent weeks progress through Physics (Week 3, Week 4, Week 5) and Generative techniques (Weeks 6 through 9). Weeks 10 introduces an interview with Alan Zucconi regarding graphics in video games and an interview with Andy Lomas on graphics in films and art. The curriculum then progresses into advanced technical fields, including 3D graphics (Weeks 11 and 12). Weeks 13 and 14 focus on the concepts of Colours, images, and pixels. Further specialization occurs in Image processing (Weeks 15 and 16) and advanced topics like Computer Vision (Weeks 17, 18, and 19). The course structure includes multiple practical assessments, such as a mid-term programming assignment submission in Week 10. Students must complete a practice quiz and a JavaScript Bundler Tool Lab during this period. The syllabus also includes dedicated instructional content like "What is Studiosity?" and "What does the feedback cover." The final graded assignment submission is scheduled for Week 20, completing the comprehensive academic journey.

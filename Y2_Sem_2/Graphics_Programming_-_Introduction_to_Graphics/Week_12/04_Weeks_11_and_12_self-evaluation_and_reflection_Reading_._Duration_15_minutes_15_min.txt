# Weeks 11 and 12 self-evaluation and reflection

. Duration: 15 minutes

The primary goal of this exercise is to promote continuous assessment of the student's understanding and capabilities against established learning outcomes. This process encourages deep reflection on the entire learning journey throughout the course. By engaging in this self-evaluation, students can effectively pinpoint specific areas needing deeper knowledge or improved skills. The resulting identification of gaps will guide the development of a concrete and actionable plan for self-improvement. Specifically, the checklist mandates the evaluation of concepts and tasks covered during Weeks 11 and 12 of the curriculum. Students are advised to revisit relevant lecture videos, readings, and activities if they lack confidence in any assessed concepts or tasks. The technical focus centers heavily on the foundational principles of 3D graphics. A key foundational concept is distinguishing the fundamental differences between 2D and 3D graphics. It is crucial to explain how depth and perspective fundamentally influence the rendering and perception within 3D scenes. Understanding the functions of a 3D renderer is paramount for practical application. This renderer is responsible for processing complex inputs, including environmental lights, object materials, and applied textures. Furthermore, the student must demonstrate the ability to identify and utilize basic 3D primitives to construct simple yet effective shapes and structures. Experimentation with adding lights to the 3D scene is a required skill set. Through this process, students must understand how various sources of lighting dramatically affect the final appearance of rendered objects. Achieving realistic results requires the application of different materials and textures to objects. Students are expected to successfully create simple 3D animations using these integrated elements—primitives, lights, and materials. Manipulation of the viewer’s perspective requires adjusting critical camera parameters. It is necessary to explain how deliberate camera positioning and changes in perspective can significantly enhance the visual impact of a 3D animation. Ultimately, the objective is to feel confident implementing basic 3D animations through the integration of diverse elements. This integration must seamlessly combine lighting effects, material properties, and carefully chosen camera angles. This comprehensive checklist serves as a robust self-assessment tool for advanced 3D visualization techniques.

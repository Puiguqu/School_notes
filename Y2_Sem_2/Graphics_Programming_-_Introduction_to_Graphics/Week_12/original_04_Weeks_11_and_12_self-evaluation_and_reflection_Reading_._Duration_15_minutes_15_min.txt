As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Week 11 and 12. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge. Self-Evaluation Checklist: I can explain the key differences between 2D and 3D graphics, including how depth and perspective influence 3D scenes. I understand the functions of a 3D renderer, including how it processes lights, materials, and textures. I can identify and use basic 3D primitives to create simple shapes and structures. I have experimented with adding lights to a 3D scene and understand how lighting affects the appearance of objects. I can apply different materials and textures to objects to achieve realistic effects. I have successfully created simple 3D animations using primitives, lights, and materials. I can adjust camera parameters to manipulate the viewer’s perspective in a 3D scene. I explain how camera positioning and perspective changes can enhance the visual impact of a 3D animation. I feel confident implementing basic 3D animations by integrating different elements such as lighting, materials, and camera angles.

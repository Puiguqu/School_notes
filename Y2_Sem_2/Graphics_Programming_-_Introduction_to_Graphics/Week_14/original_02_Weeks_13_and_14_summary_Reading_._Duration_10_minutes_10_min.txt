# Weeks 13 and 14 summary
Reading•
. Duration: 10 minutes
10 min

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-graphics-programming/supplement/O0YO1/weeks-13-and-14-summary

In this week, you have been introduced to the fundamentals of colour and image processing, focusing on digital representations of colours and images. You explored colour theory, understanding how colours interact and can be generated procedurally. Additionally, you have learned about two primary types of digital graphics, such as bitmap and vector, highlighting their differences in terms of scalability, quality, and pixel dependency. Practical applications included utilising images from various sources, such as hard disks and webcams, and working with individual pixels to modify and manipulate image data directly. By the end of this, you should have gained skills in distinguishing graphic types, generating colours using colour theory, and accessing and altering pixel information within images.

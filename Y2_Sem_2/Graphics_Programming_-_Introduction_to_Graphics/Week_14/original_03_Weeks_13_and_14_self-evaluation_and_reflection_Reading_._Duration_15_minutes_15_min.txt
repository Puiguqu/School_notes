# Weeks 13 and 14 self-evaluation and reflection
Reading•
. Duration: 15 minutes
15 min

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-graphics-programming/supplement/KrYNK/weeks-13-and-14-self-evaluation-and-reflection

As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Weeks 13 and 14. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge. Self-Evaluation Checklist: I can explain the difference between bitmap and vector graphics, including their characteristics, advantages, limitations, and appropriate use cases. I understand and can apply the principles of colour theory to create colours procedurally, considering aspects like hue, saturation, brightness, and contrast. I am able to use images from both the hard disk and webcam effectively within my projects, demonstrating an understanding of file formats and resolution requirements. I can access and manipulate pixels directly in an image, making adjustments to colour, brightness, and other attributes at the pixel level. I can differentiate between various colour models (such as RGB, CMYK, and HSL) and understand when and why to use each model. I am comfortable working with software tools and techniques that allow for pixel manipulation, understanding how these tools impact image quality and data size. I have experimented with combining bitmap and vector graphics to understand their compatibility and visual effects in digital compositions. I can explain how pixels form the basis of digital images and demonstrate an understanding of pixel density, resolution, and how they affect image clarity. I can explain the concept of procedural generation of colours and images and can implement basic procedural techniques. I have gained hands-on experience with capturing images from a webcam, understanding the basic principles of real-time image capture and processing. I can identify common issues in digital graphics, such as aliasing or pixilation, and describe techniques to address or minimize these effects. I am confident in my ability to utilise colour and image theory knowledge in practical applications, such as creating visuals for digital media or projects.

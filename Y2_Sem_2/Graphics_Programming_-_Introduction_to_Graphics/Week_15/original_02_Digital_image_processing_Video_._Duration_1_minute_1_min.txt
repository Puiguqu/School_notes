Some screen readers may require using CTRL in conjunction with the alt key Hi, and welcome to another week of graphics programming. This week we're going to be exploring digital image processing as we continue to look further into all things we can do by accessing and manipulating pixels, the building blocks of bitmap images. What is digital image processing? It is the use of computer algorithms to process digital images, usually to enhance the image or to extract useful information from it. It is a very active field and also a sub-field of digital signal processing, with many fields of science contributing to it. The applications of digital image processing are many. From restoring, sharpening images to medical imaging for detecting diseases, encoding and transmitting images over the web, just like you do with services like YouTube and Netflix, to computer vision, pattern recognition and more. What we will learn in this topic will prove very helpful in the computer vision topic because computer vision is often performed by stacking together some of the image processing techniques we are going to see today. In the next couple of videos, we'll be focusing on using an image as input, performing some calculations, and outputting a new image.

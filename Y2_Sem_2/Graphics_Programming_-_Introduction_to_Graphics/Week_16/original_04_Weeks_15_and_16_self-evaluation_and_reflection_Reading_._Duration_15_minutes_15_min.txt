As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Weeks 15 and 16. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge. 1. Understanding Image Processing Basics I can explain what image processing is. I can identify common applications of image processing in real-world scenarios. I understand the difference between basic and advanced image manipulation techniques. 2. Applying Basic Filters I am able to use simple filters to adjust image properties such as brightness, contrast, and sharpness. I can describe how different filters affect the appearance of an image. I can demonstrate the use of basic filters in an image manipulation software. 3. Using Convolution for Advanced Filters I can discuss the concept of convolution in the context of image processing. I can explain how convolution kernels work and affect an image. I have practiced applying advanced filters using convolution techniques. 4. Implementing Instagram-Style Filters I can implement an image filter similar to those used on Instagram. I can apply multiple adjustments to achieve a specific visual effect. I understand how various filter combinations create stylistic effects on images. 5. Practical Skills in Image Manipulation Software I feel comfortable navigating image manipulation software. I am able to locate and use different filtering tools in image software. I can apply filters effectively to create desired image outcomes. 6. Reflection and Application I can reflect on how the techniques learned could be applied in different image processing tasks. I feel confident in explaining my approach to others. I am prepared to build on this knowledge for more advanced image processing tasks in the future.

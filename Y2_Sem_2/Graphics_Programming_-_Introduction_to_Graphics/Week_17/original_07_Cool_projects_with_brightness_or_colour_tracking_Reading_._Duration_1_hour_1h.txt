Brightness tracking Adrien M and Claire B are an artist duo who run a company that designs and produces live dance shows which use computer vision to interact with projections. Here’s a link to a work of theirs that uses brightness tracking to guide letters towards the source. Using computer vision allows them to create rich interactions that would not have been possible had it all been scripted and choreographed in minute detail: https://vimeo.com/34550678 The Graffiti Research Lab is an artists' cooperative which claims on their site to be “dedicated to outfitting graffiti artists with open source technologies for urban communication”. They used brightness tracking to track a laser pointer on a building and to project graffiti where that laser marker is located. Here are two very old videos showing their work. Apologies for the poor quality, but you get the idea. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zOi5xinMAUQ https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DKbtTPYZEig Aurora Mechanica set up a bird cage and when the bird cage was moved a projector projected fleeing birds. It’s still works with brightness tracking only this time instead of tracking the brightest pixels using a normal camera they are using an infrared camera. On top of the cage they also installed an infrared LED. The audience does not see the LED light but the camera does and it can send commands to the projection software to release birds. Smoke and mirrors! https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KMyadNVgi34 Colour tracking Zach Lieberman is one of the lead developers for openFrameworks, a C++ based framework for developing creative applications, much like p5js. He worked on a commercial project where a car was used to generate a new typeset (a new font).

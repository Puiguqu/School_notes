The transcript introduces a related technique: Frame Differencing. This technique is designed specifically for detecting movement, distinguishing it from background subtraction which targets objects. Frame differencing is described as a straightforward method, similar in concept to background subtraction. Its key distinction, however, is that it compares the current frame not to a static background image, but to the immediately preceding frame. This comparison involves looping through the pixels of the two images for pixel-to-pixel measurement. The analysis measures differences in color values to identify movement. A significant difference in color saturation or intensity indicates movement occurring at that pixel location. Conversely, little or no difference confirms the pixel remained stationary.

Technically, the concept of movement detection can be visualized through image subtraction, resulting in a white area where movement was detected. For frame differencing to function accurately, two critical prerequisites must be met. Firstly, the environment must maintain relatively stable lighting conditions. Secondly, the camera used for capture must be stationary in order to successfully isolate and detect the motion of objects within the scene.

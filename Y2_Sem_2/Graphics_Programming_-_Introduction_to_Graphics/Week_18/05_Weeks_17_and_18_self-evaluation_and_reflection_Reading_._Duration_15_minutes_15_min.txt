Self-assessment is a crucial element of progressing through the course curriculum. It requires regularly evaluating one's understanding and capabilities against predefined learning outcomes. This structured exercise is specifically designed to facilitate deep reflection on the overall learning journey. The primary objectives are identifying areas requiring knowledge consolidation and developing a targeted improvement plan. Learners must utilize the provided checklist to meticulously evaluate their understanding of all Week 6 lessons. A key instructional guideline states that if any concept or task lacks confidence, students must revisit supplementary materials. These resources include relevant lecture videos, assigned readings, and practical activities. The assessment process begins with a foundational understanding of Computer Vision itself. Defining computer vision is essential to grasping its core purpose. Key applications of CV include advanced object recognition systems. Furthermore, it is foundational to developing autonomous vehicles. Surveillance technologies also represent a significant CV application. Recognizing these applications helps define the scope of the field. However, computer vision faces notable technical challenges. Specific limitations include dealing with variable lighting conditions. Computational resource constraints also present major hurdles in the field.

The second area of assessment focuses on Colour Tracking. This technique involves monitoring and following objects specifically based on their inherent color properties. Students must be able to discuss the practical concept of colour tracking. Describing its mechanisms allows for improved system design. Assessing this module also requires listing its specific advantages. Equally important is documenting the inherent limitations when implementing colour tracking. The third core topic is Background Subtraction. This method is utilized to isolate and distinguish moving objects within a captured scene. It is crucial to explain *how* this isolation process occurs. Learners should understand the specific scenarios where background subtraction excels. Critically, the method also possesses definite limitations within complex environmental settings.

Fourth, the discussion turns to Frame Differencing. This technique is described by comparing a sequence of consecutive video frames. By comparing frames, the system tracks movement and changes within the scene. This comparison mechanism allows for practical motion detection applications. Overall application serves as the culmination of the learned material. Students must feel confident in applying core CV principles to analyze complex visual data. This module requires the ability to connect disparate techniques into one cohesive framework. Specifically, connecting colour tracking, background subtraction, and frame differencing is paramount. This integration demonstrates understanding in real-world scenario analysis. Proficiency in these linked techniques marks a high level of mastery in the field.

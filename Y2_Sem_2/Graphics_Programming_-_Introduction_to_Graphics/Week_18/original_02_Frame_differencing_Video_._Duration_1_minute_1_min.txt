If background subtraction is for detecting objects in the scene, frame differencing is for detecting movement. It's a pretty straightforward technique and similar to background subtraction. However, instead of comparing the latest frame to the stored background image depicting the empty scene, we compare it to the previous frame. When I mean compare, I mean that we once again loop over the pixels of the two images and we do pixel-to-pixel comparisons, measuring the differences in color. Where there's a big difference, it means there's movement in that pixel. When this little or no difference, it means there is no movement in that pixel. So here in the previous frame, all pixels were white and suddenly a black object appears. We subtract one image from the other and we'll get to see in this case in white, where the movement is.

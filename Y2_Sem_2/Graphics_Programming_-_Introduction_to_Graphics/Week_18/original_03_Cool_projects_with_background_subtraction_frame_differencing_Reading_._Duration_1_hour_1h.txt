Background subtraction Random Quark is the creative studio I co-founded and direct here in London, along with two close collaborators. A few years ago we did Orthographies: a performance piece to celebrate the annual public book awards in Athens, Greece. We used background subtraction to track the performer on stage and to allow for her to interact with projections in real time. Notice how we used a lot of the techniques you have learned in this course. https://www.randomquark.com/work/book-awards Golan Levin is an artist and educator living in Pittsburgh, USA. He’s a professor at Carnegie Mellon University in the United States and the director of the Studio for Creative Inquiry. A few years ago he came to London’s Institute of Contemporary Art (ICA) with Messa di Voce, a piece in which he explored the relationship between sound and image. He and his collaborators used background subtraction in order to extract the location of the performers on stage. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=STRMcmj-gHc Frame differencing Memo Akten is one of the lead contributors to openFrameworks, a C++ based framework for developing creative applications, much like p5js. He studied for many years here at Goldsmiths, University of London. Here we see Webcam piano, a nice little hack for interacting with a musical instrument using a digital camera.

# Weeks 17 and 18 summary

During these two weeks, you explored the foundational concepts of computer vision, delving into its applications and challenges. These weeks focused on understanding how computers interpret and analyse visual information from the real world. Key principles such as colour tracking and background subtraction were introduced, providing insights into how these techniques are used in various computer vision systems. You learned to explain the concept of computer vision, including its diverse applications, such as object recognition, autonomous vehicles, and surveillance, as well as the challenges it faces, like lighting conditions and computational complexity. The concept of colour tracking was described as a method for following objects based on their colour properties, while background subtraction was examined as a technique for isolating moving objects by distinguishing them from a static background. In addition, frame differencing was used to track movement, demonstrating how the changes between consecutive video frames can highlight dynamic elements in a scene. These practical applications and theoretical insights provided you with a robust foundation in the basic principles of computer vision.

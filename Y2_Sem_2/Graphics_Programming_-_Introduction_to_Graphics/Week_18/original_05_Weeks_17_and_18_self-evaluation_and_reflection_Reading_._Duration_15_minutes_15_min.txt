As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Week 6. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge. 1. Understanding Computer Vision I can explain what computer vision is and describe its purpose. I understand the key applications of computer vision, such as object recognition, autonomous vehicles, and surveillance. I can identify the main challenges in computer vision, such as lighting conditions and computational limitations. 2. Colour Tracking I can discuss the concept of colour tracking and how it is used to follow objects based on their colour properties. I can describe the advantages and limitations of using colour tracking in computer vision systems. 3. Background Subtraction I can explain what background subtraction is and how it is used to isolate moving objects in a scene. I understand the scenarios in which background subtraction is effective and its limitations in complex environments. 4. Frame Differencing I can describe how frame differencing works to track movement by comparing consecutive video frames. I am able to use frame differencing in a practical application to detect motion. 5. Overall Application and Understanding I feel confident in applying the principles of computer vision to analyse visual data. I can connect the techniques of colour tracking, background subtraction, and frame differencing to real-world scenarios.

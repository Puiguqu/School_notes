As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Week 19. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge. 1. Understanding and Application I can explain the purpose and benefits of using a computer vision library like OpenCV. I can describe at least two advanced computer vision algorithms and their use cases. I understand how optical flow works and its applications in tracking motion within video frames. I am familiar with face recognition techniques and their practical implementation. 2. Practical Skills I successfully used optical flow to control objects on a canvas. I utilised a computer vision library to process and analyse visual data. I tracked points of interest in an image or video using appropriate methods. I implemented at least one advanced computer vision algorithm in a project or example. 3. Reflection and Improvement I feel confident in applying the concepts I learned to new computer vision challenges. I identified areas where I need further practice or review. I can articulate how these techniques could be used in real-world applications.

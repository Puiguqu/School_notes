1. The core concept of vector manipulation involves multiplication and division by a scalar value, $s$.
2. When multiplying a vector, we are essentially scaling it by the scalar amount $s$, meaning every component is adjusted by that same value.
3. Division of a vector by a scalar is structurally similar to multiplication and constitutes a process of scalar scaling.
4. The demonstration shows that vector scaling effectively adjusts the vector's length without changing its direction.
5. For example, the function `mouse.mult(0.5)` halves the vector, demonstrating scaling by 0.5.
6. Similarly, using `mouse.div(2)` achieves the same halving effect, thereby illustrating the equivalence of the two operations.

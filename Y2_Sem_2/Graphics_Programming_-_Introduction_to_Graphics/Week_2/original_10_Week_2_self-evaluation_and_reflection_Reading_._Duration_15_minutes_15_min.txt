By the end of this week, you should able to describe how vectors work, apply vector arithmetic to solve movement-related problems, and implement simple systems using vectors to create dynamic, animated scenes. As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. 1. Understanding of Vectors I can explain what a vector is and how it represents both direction and magnitude. I understand the role of vectors in representing movement in animation and physics-based systems. 2. Vector Arithmetic I can perform basic vector operations such as addition and subtraction. I am comfortable with scaling vectors to modify their magnitude. I can combine vectors to influence direction and speed effectively. 3. Acceleration and Movement I understand how vectors affect acceleration in movement. I can describe how vector changes impact simulated movement in animation. I can apply vector arithmetic to create realistic acceleration effects in animations. 4. Implementation Skills I can implement simple systems using vectors in a programming or animation environment. I am able to create basic animated scenes or simulations that use vector-based movement. I can troubleshoot and modify vector-based systems to achieve desired movement effects. 5. Reflection Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Week 2. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge.

By the end of this week, you should be able to explain how forces work, apply physics concepts in animation settings, and implement basic physics systems that incorporated realistic movements and interactions. As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. I can explain how different forces, including gravity and friction, affect objects in various scenarios. I understand the basic principles of gravity and how it influences objects in a physical system. I can describe the role of friction and its impact on motion in physical simulations. I am able to identify particles and understand their role in simulating realistic physical interactions. I can explain the basics of collision detection and its importance in digital simulations. I am confident in using physics concepts, such as gravity and friction, in animation scenarios. I have successfully implemented a simple physics system that incorporates forces, gravity, and friction. I can set up basic collision detection to manage interactions between objects in a digital environment. I feel prepared to apply these physics concepts to more complex animation or simulation projects. I have a clear understanding of how to build upon these foundational physics principles in the upcoming coursework 1. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge.

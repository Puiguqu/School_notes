By the end of this week, you should be able to describe the purpose of physics engines and explain how they simulate forces, collisions, and interactions between objects. As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. 1. Understanding Physics Engines I can explain what a physics engine is and describe its purpose in digital environments. I understand how physics engines simulate forces, collisions, and interactions between objects. 2. Knowledge of Matter.js Basics I can identify the basic elements of *Matter.js*, including bodies, constraints, and composites. I explain the function of each element within *Matter.js* and how they contribute to creating realistic physics effects. 3. Implementing Physics Systems I have successfully implemented a simple physics system using *Matter.js*. I am able to use constraints to connect bodies and control their interactions. I can create composite objects and manage interactions between multiple components in *Matter.js*. 4. Troubleshooting and Debugging I can troubleshoot basic issues when setting up physics simulations in *Matter.js*. I understand how to adjust properties (such as mass, friction, and restitution) to achieve desired physics effects. 5. Applying Knowledge to Real-World Scenarios I feel confident applying what I learned about *Matter.js* to create dynamic, interactive physics-based animations. I can identify potential applications of *Matter.js* in projects involving physics simulations. 6. Reflection Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Week 4. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge.

# Week 5 self-evaluation and reflection

By the end of this week, you should be able to describe the purpose and function of physics engines, identify and explain the essential elements of Matter.js, and implement basic physics systems using the framework. This provided a foundation for creating more complex physical interactions and simulations in code. As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. 1. Understanding Physics Engines I can describe what a physics engine is. I understand how physics engines simulate real-world physics in digital environments. I can explain why a physics engine, like Matter.js, is useful in game development or interactive simulations. 2. Matter.js Basics I am familiar with the key components of Matter.js, including bodies, constraints, and composites. I understand how each of these components contributes to building a physics-based simulation. I can identify the role of static objects within Matter.js and how they differ from dynamic bodies. 3. Implementing Physics Systems I am able to use Matter.js to implement static and dynamic bodies in a scene. I can create composites and understand how they allow for more complex physical structures. I have experimented with constraints to connect bodies and manage their interactions within the simulation. 4. Problem-Solving and Debugging I can troubleshoot and debug basic issues when implementing physics simulations in Matter.js. I feel confident modifying parameters to achieve desired effects in the physics system. 5. Application and Creativity I have created a simple physics system and a simulation using Matter.js components in the Angry birds task and submitted it for peer review I am able to explain the choices I made in building my simulation and how they contributed to realistic physics behaviour. 6. Reflection Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Week 5. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge.

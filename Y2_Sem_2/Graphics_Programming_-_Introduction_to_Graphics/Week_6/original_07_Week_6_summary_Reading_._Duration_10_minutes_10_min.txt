# Week 6 summary

In this week we focused on generative art, specifically exploring the concepts of randomness and Perlin noise. You learned that generative art involves the use of algorithms and systems to create designs, often with an element of unpredictability. By examining key characteristics of generative art, such as its reliance on autonomous systems, iterative processes, and the role of the artist as a facilitator rather than a creator, you gained a deeper understanding of this artistic approach. Through hands-on application, you practiced using randomness and noise to develop simple generative systems. Randomness introduced variability and spontaneity, while Perlin noise added a more natural, smooth randomness to their creations, giving a unique texture and flow to the patterns. By the end of the week, you should be able to explain generative art’s fundamentals, identify its essential characteristics, and utilise randomness and noise to craft your own generative designs.

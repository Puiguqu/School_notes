As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Week 6. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge. 1. Understanding Generative Art and Design I can clearly explain what generative art is. I understand the role of algorithms and autonomous systems in generative art. I can identify the differences between traditional art and generative art. 2. Recognizing Key Characteristics of Generative Art I can list and describe important characteristics of generative art, such as unpredictability and the artist’s role as a facilitator. I understand how iteration and autonomous systems contribute to the creative process in generative design. 3. Applying Randomness and Noise I can apply randomness to create variability within generative systems. I understand the concept of Perlin noise and its use in producing natural, smooth variations. I have practiced using both randomness and Perlin noise to influence visual outcomes in my generative designs. 4. Creating Simple Generative Systems I have created at least one simple generative system using randomness. I have created at least one simple generative system using Perlin noise. I can discuss how randomness and noise impact the visual and structural properties of generative art.

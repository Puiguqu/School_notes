# Week 7 summary

In this week, you explored the principles of generative art, focusing on how randomness and noise can be harnessed in both 2D and 3D environments to create unique, algorithm-driven designs. You began by understanding what generative art and design entail, recognising it as a form of art created through systems and algorithms. Key characteristics of generative art, such as its reliance on predefined rules, unpredictability, and the influence of random or pseudo-random inputs, were discussed. You then applied these concepts, using randomness and noise functions to build basic generative systems. This included experimenting with various noise techniques to simulate natural textures and patterns, as well as utilising randomness to introduce variation and complexity into digital art forms. Through these exercises, you gained a practical understanding of how to control and implement these elements to achieve desired generative outcomes.

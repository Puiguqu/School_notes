As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Week 7. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge. 1. Understanding Generative Art I can clearly define what generative art and design are. I can identify the role of systems and algorithms in creating generative art. I understand how generative art is distinct from other forms of digital art. 2. Key Characteristics of Generative Art I can list and explain key characteristics of generative art, such as the use of rules, unpredictability, and randomness. I understand the importance of randomness and noise in creating variety and complexity in generative art. I can discuss how different noise techniques influence the appearance of generative artwork. 3. Application of Randomness and Noise I successfully applied randomness to introduce variation into a generative system. I can distinguish between 2D and 3D noise and understand how each is used in generative systems. I used noise to simulate natural textures or patterns in a generative design. I experimented with controlling randomness and noise to produce specific visual effects. 4. Reflection and Improvement I can identify areas where I struggled and have noted strategies to improve my understanding. I feel confident in applying randomness and noise to create generative art, or I know what areas to focus on for further practice.

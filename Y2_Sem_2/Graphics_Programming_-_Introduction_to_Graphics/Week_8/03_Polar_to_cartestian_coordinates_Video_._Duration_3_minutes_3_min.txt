5. However, an alternative method for locating a point in 2D space is using polar coordinates.
6. In the polar coordinate system, a point is determined by two values: the distance from the origin, and an angle from the polar axis.
7. The distance from the origin is called the radial coordinate, often denoted by $r$ or radial distance.
8. The second parameter is the angle itself, which is called the polar angle and is represented by $\theta$.
9. Angles in polar notation can be expressed in either degrees or radians, where $2\pi$ radians equals 360 degrees.
10. Polar coordinates are most appropriate when the phenomenon under consideration is inherently tied to direction and length from a center point.
11. Examples illustrating this include the Archimedean spiral, which is simpler to express using polar formulas.
12. Furthermore, many physical systems, such as celestial bodies orbiting a central point, are more easily modeled with polar coordinates.
13. For instance, modeling planets orbiting the sun is more intuitive using polar coordinates than a Cartesian system.
14. Since programming environments like p5 utilize Cartesian coordinates for primitives, a conversion method is required.
15. This conversion process leverages trigonometry, specifically the sine and cosine functions.
16. Sine of an angle $\theta$ is defined as the opposite side divided by the hypotenuse.
17. When considering a point $(x, y)$ and radius $r$, the relationship yields $\sin(\theta) = y/r$.
18. By rearranging this trigonometric identity to solve for $y$, we obtain the formula $y = r \sin(\theta)$.
19. This formula dictates how to calculate the y-coordinate when only polar coordinates $(r, \theta)$ are given.
20. Similarly, the cosine of the angle relates the adjacent side, $x$, to the hypotenuse, $r$.
21. The definition for cosine is $\cos(\theta) = x/r$.
22. Solving this identity for $x$ yields the formula $x = r \cos(\theta)$.
23. These two derived formulas, $y = r \sin(\theta)$ and $x = r \cos(\theta)$, are crucial for coordinate conversion.

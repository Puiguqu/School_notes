Before we go any further, let's begin by refreshing our knowledge of trigonometry, as this is going to help us tackle various problems and enable us to do some really cool things. Let's start off by considering the meaning of sine, cosine and tangent. We'll then see how this can be used to draw something on the screen in terms of polar coordinates. No need to shut off your computer in panic, I promise this will be a gentle refresher of basic concepts and provide us with just enough to help us be creative. Here's a right triangle, which is a triangle with an angle that is 90 degrees, and another angle, let's call that theta. Opposite to this angle theta, we have a side which we will call opposite and adjacent side which connects point A to point C. And of course the hypotenuse of this triangle, which connects point A to point B. Sine of theta is given by the length of the opposite side divided by the length of the hypotenuse. Cosine of theta is given by the adjacent divided by the hypotenuse. And finally, tangent is the opposite divided by the adjacent. Now there is a link in the first place between the angle theta and the lengths of the sides should start to become a little bit obvious. For example, the bigger the angle theta, the longer the opposite side. If this is difficult to remember, you can use a little mnemonic called SOHCAHTOA, which contains all the logic in condensed form.

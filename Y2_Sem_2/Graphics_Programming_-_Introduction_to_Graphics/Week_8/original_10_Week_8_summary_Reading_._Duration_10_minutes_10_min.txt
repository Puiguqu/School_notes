# Week 8 summary
Reading•
. Duration: 10 minutes

URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/uol-graphics-programming/supplement/Swhas/week-8-summary

In this week, you explored the foundational concepts of trigonometry, oscillation, and additive synthesis as applied to generative art and design. You examined how polar coordinates can be used to create intricate shapes and patterns, leveraging trigonometric functions to bring these shapes to life. Oscillation was introduced as a method to code movement, allowing you to simulate natural, rhythmic motions within their designs. Additionally, you studied additive synthesis, applying it to build generative systems that layer waveforms to create complex, evolving patterns. The topic also covered recursive systems, equipping you with the skills to create fractal-like structures that exhibit self-similarity and depth. By the end of the module, you had hands-on experience implementing these techniques, combining mathematical principles with coding to generate unique, dynamic visual compositions.

As you progress through your course, it is crucial to regularly assess your understanding and capabilities against the learning outcomes. This exercise is designed to help you reflect on your learning journey, identify areas where you may need to deepen your knowledge or skills, and develop a plan for improvement. Use this checklist to evaluate your understanding of the lessons covered in Week 8. If you don't feel confident about any of these concepts/tasks, revisit the relevant lecture videos, readings and activities to help you consolidate your knowledge. 1. Trigonometry and Shape Creation I understand the principles of trigonometry as they apply to shape creation in design. I successfully used trigonometric functions to create shapes. I can apply polar coordinates to create complex patterns in generative art. 2. Oscillation and Movement I understand the concept of oscillation and how it applies to coding movement. I implemented oscillation to simulate rhythmic or natural movement in code. I can adjust oscillation parameters to change the speed, amplitude, or frequency of movement. 3. Additive Synthesis and Generative Systems I understand the concept of additive synthesis and how it applies to generative art. I successfully implemented additive synthesis to layer waveforms and create dynamic patterns. I can explain how different waveforms combine to form complex designs. 4. Recursion and Fractals I understand the concept of recursion and how it’s used to create fractals. I created fractal structures using recursive code. I can explain the properties of fractals and how self-similarity is achieved through recursion. 5. Overall Understanding and Application I feel confident in my ability to use trigonometry, oscillation, additive synthesis, and recursion in generative art projects. I can troubleshoot and refine my code to improve design outcomes. I am able to explain my design process and the mathematical concepts behind my generative art pieces.
